Day 10 Change & Process Verbs — Getting Things Done Words 451–494 · 44 words
expand /ɪkˈspænd/ v.
To become or make larger in size, number, or scope.

Daily: The restaurant expanded into three new locations this year.

Meeting: Our plan is to expand the product's feature set in Q2.

transform /trænsˈfɔːrm/ v.
To change completely in form or character.

Daily: Daily exercise transformed both my body and my mindset.

Meeting: This initiative will transform how we deliver services to clients.

convert /kənˈvɜːrt/ v.
To change from one form, purpose, or belief to another.

Daily: We converted the spare bedroom into a home office.

Meeting: The goal is to convert free trial users into paying subscribers.

replace /rɪˈpleɪs/ v.
To take the place of something or someone.

Daily: I need to replace the batteries in the remote control.

Meeting: We're planning to replace the legacy system with a modern cloud solution.

remove /rɪˈmuːv/ v.
To take something away from a place.

Daily: Please remove your shoes before entering the house.

Meeting: We need to remove this unnecessary step from the approval workflow.

combine /kəmˈbaɪn/ v.
To join two or more things together.

Daily: Combine flour, butter, and eggs to make the perfect pastry.

Meeting: Let's combine the two workstreams to avoid duplication of effort.

connect /kəˈnekt/ v.
To join or link things together; to relate to.

Daily: We connected the new printer to the Wi-Fi network.

Meeting: How does this initiative connect to our overall company strategy?

divide /dɪˈvaɪd/ v.
To separate into parts; to share between people.

Daily: Let's divide the chores between all the housemates.

Meeting: We'll divide the project into three phases for easier management.

compare /kəmˈpeər/ v.
To examine how two things are similar or different.

Daily: It's hard to compare the two restaurants — both are excellent.

Meeting: Let's compare the current process with the proposed new approach.

calculate /ˈkælkjuleɪt/ v.
To determine an amount by using mathematics.

Daily: I calculated the tip in my head before paying the bill.

Meeting: The finance team will calculate the total ROI for this initiative.

estimate /ˈestɪmeɪt/ v.
To roughly calculate or judge a value.

Daily: I estimated it would take two hours but it took four.

Meeting: Can you estimate the development effort for this new feature?

predict /prɪˈdɪkt/ v.
To say what will happen in the future based on information.

Daily: It's hard to predict what the weather will do at this time of year.

Meeting: Based on current trends, we can predict a 15% increase in demand.

forecast /ˈfɔːrkæst/ v.
To estimate future trends, especially in weather or business.

Daily: The meteorologist forecast sunshine for the entire long weekend.

Meeting: The sales team has forecast a 20% increase in revenue for Q4.

arrange /əˈreɪndʒ/ v.
To put things in order; to plan and organize.

Daily: I arranged the flowers beautifully in a tall glass vase.

Meeting: Could you arrange a meeting room for the vendor demo on Tuesday?

prepare /prɪˈpeər/ v.
To make ready for use or a specific purpose.

Daily: I prepare my lunches for the whole week every Sunday evening.

Meeting: Each presenter must prepare their slides at least 48 hours in advance.

finish /ˈfɪnɪʃ/ v.
To complete or bring to an end.

Daily: Did you finish reading that novel I lent you last month?

Meeting: We need to finish the user acceptance testing by Friday afternoon.

start /stɑːrt/ v.
To begin or cause something to begin.

Daily: What time does the film start? I don't want to be late.

Meeting: Let's start the meeting by reviewing last week's action items.

revise /rɪˈvaɪz/ v.
To change or correct something to make it better.

Daily: I stayed up late revising my essay before the final submission.

Meeting: Please revise the proposal based on the client's feedback and resend.

upgrade /ˈʌpɡreɪd/ v.
To raise to a higher standard; to improve.

Daily: I upgraded my phone and the improvement is remarkable.

Meeting: We need to upgrade the server infrastructure before the new release.

fix /fɪks/ v.
To repair something; to make permanent or definite.

Daily: He fixed the leaking tap in under ten minutes.

Meeting: The engineering team will fix the critical bug overnight.

affect /əˈfekt/ v.
To have an influence on; to make a difference to.

Daily: A poor night's sleep really affects my ability to concentrate.

Meeting: This change will significantly affect the timeline for delivery.

influence /ˈɪnfluəns/ v.
To have an effect on the development or behavior of something.

Daily: My grandmother influenced my love of cooking more than anyone.

Meeting: Customer feedback has directly influenced our product roadmap decisions.

impact /ˈɪmpækt/ v.
To have a strong effect on something.

Daily: Screen time before bed negatively impacts my sleep quality.

Meeting: How will this decision impact the teams working downstream?

prevent /prɪˈvent/ v.
To stop something from happening.

Daily: Regular maintenance helps prevent costly breakdowns.

Meeting: We need to identify what steps will prevent this issue from recurring.

contribute /kənˈtrɪbjuːt/ v.
To give or add to something.

Daily: Everyone contributed something delicious to the potluck dinner.

Meeting: Each team member is expected to contribute to the quarterly retrospective.

generate /ˈdʒenəreɪt/ v.
To produce or create something.

Daily: This solar panel generates enough energy for the whole house.

Meeting: The campaign generated over 5,000 qualified leads in the first month.

collect /kəˈlekt/ v.
To gather things together; to receive payment.

Daily: She collects vintage postcards from every city she visits.

Meeting: We will collect user feedback after the beta release next week.

gather /ˈɡæðər/ v.
To bring together or accumulate in one place.

Daily: The whole family gathered around the fire after dinner.

Meeting: Let's gather all the requirements before we start design work.

transfer /ˈtrænsf3ːr/ v.
To move from one place, person, or situation to another.

Daily: She transferred all her savings into a higher-interest account.

Meeting: We will transfer the data to the new system during the maintenance window.

integrate /ˈɪntɪɡreɪt/ v.
To combine or coordinate different parts into a whole.

Daily: He has fully integrated into the local community since moving there.

Meeting: We need to integrate this API before the beta launch.

optimize /ˈɒptɪmaɪz/ v.
To make something as good or effective as possible.

Daily: I optimized my morning routine so I get an extra 30 minutes of sleep.

Meeting: We should optimize the checkout flow to reduce cart abandonment.

simplify /ˈsɪmplɪfaɪ/ v.
To make something easier to understand or do.

Daily: The new navigation has simplified the whole experience.

Meeting: The goal is to simplify the approval process from five steps to two.

limit /ˈlɪmɪt/ v.
To set a maximum; to restrict something.

Daily: I'm trying to limit my social media use to one hour a day.

Meeting: We'll need to limit the scope if we want to hit the launch date.

extend /ɪkˈstend/ v.
To make longer or larger; to stretch out.

Daily: We extended our stay by two extra nights because we loved the place.

Meeting: Can we extend the deadline by one week to allow for proper testing?

adjust /əˈdʒʌst/ v.
To change slightly to make it better or more suitable.

Daily: I adjusted my alarm to allow more time in the mornings.

Meeting: We need to adjust the budget allocation before the next financial review.

adapt /əˈdæpt/ v.
To change to suit a new situation.

Daily: Cats are remarkably good at adapting to new environments.

Meeting: We'll need to adapt our strategy for each regional market.

modify /ˈmɒdɪfaɪ/ v.
To make partial changes to; to adjust.

Daily: I modified the recipe slightly and it turned out even better.

Meeting: Please modify the contract terms as discussed and resend for approval.

verify /ˈverɪfaɪ/ v.
To check or confirm that something is accurate or true.

Daily: I verified the hotel booking twice before we left for the trip.

Meeting: Please verify the figures with the finance team before we publish the report.

validate /ˈvælɪdeɪt/ v.
To confirm or prove the accuracy or value of something.

Daily: She validated her theory by running multiple experiments.

Meeting: We need to validate these assumptions with real user data.

check /tʃek/ v.
To examine something to make sure it is correct or safe.

Daily: Always check the weather forecast before packing for a trip.

Meeting: Let's check our progress against the original project plan.

finalize /ˈfaɪnəlaɪz/ v.
To complete the last stages of a plan or agreement.

Daily: We finalized the guest list for the wedding yesterday.

Meeting: We need to finalize the scope document by Friday before development begins.

deploy /dɪˈplɔɪ/ v.
To move resources or people into position; to release software.

Daily: We deployed volunteers to help with the clean-up after the storm.

Meeting: The engineering team will deploy the new release to production at midnight.

automate /ˈɔːtəmeɪt/ v.
To use technology to operate a process with minimal human input.

Daily: I automated my bill payments so I never forget a due date.

Meeting: We should automate this reporting process to save the team 10 hours a week.

accelerate /əkˈseləreɪt/ v.
To increase in speed or cause to develop faster.

Daily: Adding a new habit gradually is more effective than trying to accelerate change.

Meeting: We need to accelerate development to catch up with the original schedule.
