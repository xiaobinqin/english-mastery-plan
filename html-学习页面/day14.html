Day 14 Quantity, Measurement & Data Words Words 651–692 · 42 words
amount /əˈmaʊnt/ n.
A quantity of something; the total.

Daily: I was surprised by the amount of food at the buffet!

Meeting: Please confirm the exact amount we need to allocate from the reserve budget.

total /ˈtoʊtəl/ n.
The whole number or amount after everything is counted.

Daily: The total bill came to more than I had budgeted for.

Meeting: The total development effort is estimated at 600 person-hours.

average /ˈævərɪdʒ/ n.
A number representing the typical value in a set.

Daily: On average, I walk about 8,000 steps a day.

Meeting: The average resolution time for support tickets is currently 4.2 hours.

percentage /pərˈsentɪdʒ/ n.
A rate or proportion expressed as a fraction of 100.

Daily: What percentage of your income do you save each month?

Meeting: Conversion rate improved by a percentage of 12% after the redesign.

rate /reɪt/ n.
A measure of speed, frequency, or proportion.

Daily: The heart rate monitor shows my pulse during each workout.

Meeting: Our customer churn rate has decreased from 8% to 5% this year.

ratio /ˈreɪʃɪoʊ/ n.
The relationship between two amounts.

Daily: The golden ratio appears frequently in nature and architecture.

Meeting: Our cost-to-revenue ratio has improved considerably this quarter.

level /ˈlevəl/ n.
A position on a scale; a degree of intensity.

Daily: I try to maintain a consistent energy level throughout the day.

Meeting: Customer satisfaction levels are at their highest point in three years.

degree /dɪˈɡriː/ n.
A unit of temperature; a level or extent.

Daily: It's 30 degrees outside — perfect weather for the beach.

Meeting: The degree of complexity in this project was underestimated at the start.

scale /skeɪl/ n.
A range of values; the relative size of something.

Daily: On a scale of one to ten, how would you rate your happiness?

Meeting: We are now operating at a global scale, which changes our risk profile.

range /reɪndʒ/ n.
The limits between which something varies; a variety.

Daily: The price range at this market suits every type of shopper.

Meeting: We offer a full range of pricing options to suit different business sizes.

maximum /ˈmæksɪməm/ n.
The largest or highest amount possible.

Daily: The maximum number of passengers in this lift is eight.

Meeting: The maximum budget for this initiative has been set at $500,000.

minimum /ˈmɪnɪməm/ n.
The smallest or lowest amount possible.

Daily: The minimum age for this activity is 18 years.

Meeting: The minimum viable product must be delivered within eight weeks.

limit /ˈlɪmɪt/ n.
A point beyond which something does not continue.

Daily: There is a weight limit on this zipline for safety reasons.

Meeting: We have reached the limit of what this team can deliver without more resources.

measure /ˈmeʒər/ n.
A standard unit; a course of action; a ruler.

Daily: Adding a measure of vanilla extract makes all the difference.

Meeting: What measure will we use to determine if this initiative was successful?

unit /ˈjuːnɪt/ n.
A single item; a standard quantity used in measurement.

Daily: We sold every unit of the limited-edition product within the hour.

Meeting: The target is to sell 50,000 units within the first 90 days of launch.

quarter /ˈkwɔːrtər/ n.
One of four equal parts; a three-month business period.

Daily: We review our savings goals at the end of every quarter.

Meeting: Results for Q3 will be presented to the board next Thursday morning.

estimate /ˈestɪmɪt/ n.
An approximate calculation of value or quantity.

Daily: The builder gave us a rough estimate for the kitchen renovation.

Meeting: The time estimate for this feature is five to eight business days.

forecast /ˈfɔːrkæst/ n.
A prediction of future events, especially weather or finance.

Daily: The weather forecast promises clear skies for the entire long weekend.

Meeting: The updated sales forecast shows stronger growth than initially projected.

trend /trend/ n.
A general direction of change over time.

Daily: The trend toward plant-based eating shows no sign of slowing.

Meeting: The data clearly shows a positive trend in user retention over Q3.

figure /ˈfɪɡər/ n.
A number or statistical value; an important person.

Daily: I'm not good at memorizing figures like phone numbers.

Meeting: The figures on slide three clearly support our investment case.

statistic /stəˈtɪstɪk/ n.
A fact or piece of data obtained from a study.

Daily: That's a shocking statistic — I had no idea so many people face that issue.

Meeting: One striking statistic: 65% of customers abandon checkout at the final step.

proportion /prəˈpɔːrʃən/ n.
A part, share, or number in comparison to a whole.

Daily: A large proportion of the guests traveled from overseas.

Meeting: A disproportionate proportion of our support tickets relate to login issues.

volume /ˈvɒljuːm/ n.
The amount of space; a large quantity; a book.

Daily: Can you turn the volume down? I'm trying to concentrate.

Meeting: The volume of customer inquiries has doubled since the last product update.

frequency /ˈfriːkwənsi/ n.
The rate at which something occurs.

Daily: I've increased the frequency of my gym sessions to four times a week.

Meeting: What is the recommended frequency for these status check-ins?

distribution /ˌdɪstrɪˈbjuːʃən/ n.
The way something is shared or spread; the process of delivery.

Daily: The uneven distribution of tasks caused some frustration in the team.

Meeting: The distribution channel strategy must be finalized before the launch.

variance /ˈveərɪəns/ n.
The degree of difference from a standard; a statistical measure.

Daily: The variance in my commute time is huge depending on the traffic.

Meeting: The budget variance report shows we are 8% over the original forecast.

index /ˈɪndeks/ n.
A list or indicator showing changes in a standard value.

Daily: I found the book's index very useful for locating specific topics.

Meeting: Our customer satisfaction index has reached a record high this quarter.

growth /ɡroʊθ/ n.
The process of increasing in size or importance.

Daily: Watching a seedling grow into a plant is surprisingly satisfying.

Meeting: Month-on-month growth of 8% puts us well ahead of our annual target.

value /ˈvæljuː/ n.
The monetary worth; the importance or usefulness of something.

Daily: I prefer buying fewer things of higher value that will last longer.

Meeting: The value we deliver to clients is our most powerful competitive advantage.

cost /kɒst/ n.
The amount of money required; the loss or sacrifice made.

Daily: The real cost of convenience is often not obvious at first.

Meeting: We need to drive down the cost per acquisition to improve our margins.

benefit /ˈbenɪfɪt/ n.
An advantage or gain from something.

Daily: One benefit of working from home is saving two hours of commuting.

Meeting: The key benefit of this approach is a 40% reduction in processing time.

return /rɪˈtɜːrn/ n.
A profit from an investment; the act of going back.

Daily: The return on this investment has exceeded all expectations.

Meeting: What is the expected return on investment for this marketing campaign?

margin /ˈmɑːrdʒɪn/ n.
The difference between revenue and cost; a spare amount.

Daily: I write notes in the margins of every book I read.

Meeting: Our gross margin has improved from 32% to 39% over the last six months.

share /ʃeər/ n.
A portion belonging to someone; a unit of ownership.

Daily: I always offer to share my dessert when eating out.

Meeting: Our market share in the premium segment has grown by 3 points.

weight /weɪt/ n.
The heaviness of an object; importance or influence.

Daily: I pack as light as possible when traveling with hand luggage only.

Meeting: We need to give more weight to user feedback in our product decisions.

size /saɪz/ n.
The extent or dimensions of something.

Daily: The size of this kitchen is perfect for a family of four.

Meeting: The size of the opportunity justifies the investment we're proposing.

length /leŋθ/ n.
The distance from one end to another; the duration.

Daily: The length of the film is three hours — bring snacks!

Meeting: The length of the onboarding process needs to be reduced significantly.

width /wɪdθ/ n.
The measurement from side to side.

Daily: I measured the width of the door before ordering the furniture.

Meeting: Ensure the table width fits within the conference room layout.

height /haɪt/ n.
The distance from the bottom to the top.

Daily: The height of the ceiling makes this room feel incredibly spacious.

Meeting: The project is now at its height — all teams are working at full capacity.

speed /spiːd/ n.
The rate at which someone or something moves.

Daily: The speed of the internet connection here is impressive.

Meeting: Delivery speed is the top factor cited by customers in our surveys.

distance /ˈdɪstəns/ n.
The length of space between two points.

Daily: I prefer to live within walking distance of a good café.

Meeting: We are still some distance away from being fully compliant with the new standards.

capacity /kəˈpæsɪti/ n.
The maximum amount or number; the ability to do something.

Daily: The venue has a capacity of 250 guests for a seated dinner.

Meeting: We need to assess our team's capacity before committing to new projects.
