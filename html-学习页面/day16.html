Day 16 Nature, Science & Environment Nouns Words 751–791 · 41 words
nature /ˈneɪtʃər/ n.
The physical world, including animals, plants, and landscapes.

Daily: Spending time in nature is the best way I know to reset my mind.

Meeting: By nature, this is a complex problem with no simple solution.

earth /ɜːrθ/ n.
The planet we live on; soil or ground.

Daily: The view of the earth from space is awe-inspiring.

Meeting: We have a responsibility to every person and team across the earth in our operations.

environment /ɪnˈvaɪrənmənt/ n.
The natural world; the conditions surrounding something.

Daily: We can all do small things every day to protect the environment.

Meeting: Creating a psychologically safe environment is essential for team creativity.

energy /ˈenədʒi/ n.
The power to do work; enthusiasm and vitality.

Daily: I get most of my daily energy from morning exercise.

Meeting: We need high energy from the whole team for the final push this week.

material /məˈtɪərɪəl/ n.
Matter from which something is made; information for use.

Daily: She uses only natural, sustainable materials in her artwork.

Meeting: Please review the training materials before the workshop on Thursday.

element /ˈelɪmənt/ n.
A basic part of something; a chemical substance.

Daily: Trust is a key element in every meaningful relationship.

Meeting: What are the critical elements of a successful product launch?

structure /ˈstrʌktʃər/ n.
The way parts are organized or connected; a building.

Daily: The beautiful timber structure of the old barn has been preserved.

Meeting: We need to review the team structure before the reorganization begins.

pattern /ˈpætərn/ n.
A repeated form; a regular way something occurs.

Daily: I noticed a pattern of waking up tired — I need to improve my sleep.

Meeting: The data reveals a concerning pattern in customer drop-off during onboarding.

network /ˈnetwɜːrk/ n.
A system of connections; a group of people in contact.

Daily: Building a strong personal network opens so many doors.

Meeting: We rely on a strong partner network to reach customers in remote regions.

connection /kəˈnekʃən/ n.
A link or relationship between people or things.

Daily: The connection between good sleep and productivity is well established.

Meeting: Our API connection to the payment gateway is causing intermittent failures.

force /fɔːrs/ n.
Power or energy; a powerful effect; armed services.

Daily: The force of the wind during the storm knocked down several trees.

Meeting: Market forces are driving rapid change across the entire industry.

pressure /ˈpreʃər/ n.
The force exerted on a surface; stress or demand.

Daily: I perform well under pressure — it brings out my best work.

Meeting: There is significant pressure from the client to deliver ahead of schedule.

temperature /ˈtempərɪtʃər/ n.
The degree of heat or cold; a measured value.

Daily: The temperature has been unusually warm for this time of year.

Meeting: We need to monitor the data center temperature closely after the upgrade.

chemical /ˈkemɪkəl/ n.
A substance produced by or used in chemistry.

Daily: I try to use natural cleaning products with fewer harsh chemicals.

Meeting: Our manufacturing process must comply with all chemical safety regulations.

organic /ɔːˈɡænɪk/ adj.
Relating to living matter; produced without artificial chemicals.

Daily: I buy organic vegetables whenever they're available and affordable.

Meeting: We take an organic approach to growing our community — no paid advertising.

resource /ˈriːsɔːrs/ n.
A stock or supply that can be used.

Daily: Water is our most precious and limited natural resource.

Meeting: We need to allocate resources more strategically across all active projects.

space /speɪs/ n.
A continuous area; room to move; the universe beyond earth.

Daily: I need more space in my morning to think and plan before work begins.

Meeting: Is there enough space in the current sprint to take on this additional task?

surface /ˈsɜːrfɪs/ n.
The outside or top layer of something.

Daily: The surface of the lake was perfectly still on that winter morning.

Meeting: On the surface, the proposal looks solid — but the details need scrutiny.

cycle /ˈsaɪkəl/ n.
A series of events that are regularly repeated.

Daily: The cycle of seasons gives each year its own distinct rhythm.

Meeting: The development cycle for each feature should not exceed four weeks.

process /ˈproʊses/ n.
A series of actions or changes; a natural series of events.

Daily: The process of making sourdough bread is slow but deeply satisfying.

Meeting: Our hiring process typically takes three to four weeks from application to offer.

reaction /riˈækʃən/ n.
A response to something; a chemical change.

Daily: Her reaction to the surprise party was absolutely priceless.

Meeting: The market's reaction to our announcement has been very positive.

effect /ɪˈfekt/ n.
A result or consequence; an impression made.

Daily: The effect of a good night's sleep on mood is remarkable.

Meeting: We need to measure the effect of this campaign on brand awareness.

cause /kɔːz/ n.
A reason for an event; a goal one believes in.

Daily: What was the cause of the traffic delay this morning?

Meeting: Root cause analysis shows the issue originated from a configuration error.

factor /ˈfæktər/ n.
A circumstance that contributes to a result.

Daily: The main factor in her success was her extraordinary work ethic.

Meeting: What are the key risk factors we need to account for in this plan?

variable /ˈveərɪəbəl/ n.
Something that can change; a factor in an experiment.

Daily: The unpredictable weather is the main variable in our hiking plans.

Meeting: We need to control for all external variables in this analysis.

condition /kənˈdɪʃən/ n.
The state of something; a requirement for something else.

Daily: The apartment was in perfect condition when we moved in.

Meeting: The contract is conditional — we proceed only if conditions are met.

experiment /ɪkˈsperɪmənt/ n.
A scientific procedure to test a hypothesis.

Daily: I'm running a personal experiment to track sleep quality this month.

Meeting: We should run an experiment with a small cohort before scaling this feature.

observation /ˌɒbzəˈveɪʃən/ n.
The act of watching carefully; a remark based on what is noticed.

Daily: Her observation that the plant needed more water saved it.

Meeting: I'd like to share a key observation from the user research sessions.

hypothesis /haɪˈpɒθəsɪs/ n.
A proposed explanation based on limited evidence.

Daily: My hypothesis is that more sleep will improve my productivity.

Meeting: Our hypothesis is that simplifying checkout will reduce abandonment by 20%.

evidence /ˈevɪdəns/ n.
Facts or information indicating something is true.

Daily: There's strong evidence that regular reading improves cognitive function.

Meeting: Before making this decision, let's review all the available evidence.

conclusion /kənˈkluːʒən/ n.
A decision reached after consideration of evidence.

Daily: After much deliberation, we came to the same conclusion independently.

Meeting: The conclusion is clear: we need to invest in user experience improvements.

theory /ˈθɪərɪ/ n.
A set of principles explaining something; a speculation.

Daily: In theory, a four-day work week should improve productivity.

Meeting: The theory is sound, but we need real-world validation before committing.

approach /əˈproʊtʃ/ n.
A way of dealing with something; the act of coming near.

Daily: I prefer a gradual approach when starting a new exercise routine.

Meeting: The current approach isn't working — we need to try something different.

technique /tekˈniːk/ n.
A skillful or efficient way of doing something.

Daily: She learned a new cooking technique at a weekend workshop.

Meeting: The technique we used for this campaign resulted in a 30% lift in conversions.

procedure /prəˈsiːdʒər/ n.
An established series of actions for accomplishing something.

Daily: The procedure for boarding the plane was surprisingly smooth.

Meeting: Please follow the standard incident response procedure when logging tickets.

accuracy /ˈækjərəsi/ n.
The quality of being correct and precise.

Daily: I double-check directions for accuracy before heading out.

Meeting: We must verify the accuracy of these figures before sharing with the board.

precision /prɪˈsɪʒən/ n.
The quality of being exact and very accurate.

Daily: The chef cut each vegetable with impressive precision.

Meeting: This type of financial modelling requires a high degree of precision.

reliability /rɪˌlaɪəˈbɪlɪti/ n.
The quality of being consistently good and dependable.

Daily: The reliability of public transport here is genuinely impressive.

Meeting: System reliability is our most important technical metric.

validity /vəˈlɪdɪti/ n.
The quality of being logically correct or legally acceptable.

Daily: I questioned the validity of the information before sharing it.

Meeting: We must test the validity of these assumptions with real user data.

sample /ˈsæmpəl/ n.
A small part used as evidence of the whole; a specimen.

Daily: Would you like to try a sample of this new cheese before buying?

Meeting: The survey sample of 300 users gives us statistically significant results.

interpretation /ɪnˌtɜːrprɪˈteɪʃən/ n.
An explanation or understanding of something.

Daily: His interpretation of the painting was completely different from mine.

Meeting: Different teams may have different interpretations of the same data.
