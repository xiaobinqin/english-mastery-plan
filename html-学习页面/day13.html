Day 13 Meeting & Project Management Nouns Words 601–645 · 45 words
meeting /ˈmiːtɪŋ/ n.
A gathering of people for a specific purpose.

Daily: I had a wonderful meeting with an old friend over coffee.

Meeting: The weekly team meeting is every Monday at 9 AM sharp.

conference /ˈkɒnfərəns/ n.
A formal meeting for discussion; a large organized event.

Daily: She attended an international nutrition conference last spring.

Meeting: The annual sales conference will be held in Singapore next March.

presentation /ˌprezənˈteɪʃən/ n.
A formal talk or display of information to an audience.

Daily: Her presentation on climate change was genuinely eye-opening.

Meeting: The board presentation needs to be concise — no more than 20 slides.

agenda /əˈdʒendə/ n.
A list of items to be discussed at a meeting.

Daily: The dinner party agenda included three courses and a game night.

Meeting: Please send the meeting agenda at least 24 hours in advance.

minutes /ˈmɪnɪts/ n.
The official written record of a meeting.

Daily: I take my own notes but also refer to the official minutes.

Meeting: Could someone volunteer to take minutes during today's meeting?

feedback /ˈfiːdbæk/ n.
Information about reactions to something; evaluation.

Daily: I welcome honest feedback — it helps me grow and improve.

Meeting: The client's feedback on the prototype was overwhelmingly positive.

discussion /dɪˈskʌʃən/ n.
A detailed conversation about a topic.

Daily: The book club discussion lasted three hours — it was fantastic.

Meeting: Can we save the discussion on budget until after the demo?

decision /dɪˈsɪʒən/ n.
A choice made after thinking about options.

Daily: Moving abroad was the best decision I ever made.

Meeting: A decision on the final design needs to be made by end of week.

priority /praɪˈɒrɪti/ n.
Something given or requiring attention before other things.

Daily: My health has become my number one priority this year.

Meeting: Quality assurance should be a top priority, not an afterthought.

outcome /ˈaʊtkʌm/ n.
A result or consequence of an action or process.

Daily: The outcome of the match was unexpected — a 3-0 win!

Meeting: What is the desired outcome of this initiative for the business?

challenge /ˈtʃælɪndʒ/ n.
Something difficult that requires effort and skill.

Daily: Learning to cook Japanese food was a fun challenge.

Meeting: The biggest challenge we face is the short timeline and limited resources.

recommendation /ˌrekəmenˈdeɪʃən/ n.
A suggestion that something is good or suitable.

Daily: I found this restaurant through a recommendation from a trusted friend.

Meeting: Our recommendation is a phased implementation over six months.

concern /kənˈsɜːrn/ n.
A feeling of worry; something of importance to someone.

Daily: My main concern is whether we'll have enough time to prepare.

Meeting: I want to address a concern raised by several team members before we proceed.

risk /rɪsk/ n.
The possibility that something bad will happen.

Daily: There's always some risk involved in trying something completely new.

Meeting: We need to assess the risk of launching without full testing coverage.

opportunity /ˌɒpəˈtjuːnɪti/ n.
A time or situation that makes something possible.

Daily: Moving to a new city was the opportunity I had been waiting for.

Meeting: This partnership represents a significant opportunity for revenue growth.

approval /əˈpruːvəl/ n.
Consent or agreement; a favorable opinion.

Daily: She received approval from her parents to study abroad.

Meeting: We need finance approval before we can commit to this expenditure.

resource /ˈriːsɔːrs/ n.
A stock or supply available for use; a person with skill.

Daily: Time and money are both finite resources — use them wisely.

Meeting: We simply don't have enough resources to take on another project right now.

role /roʊl/ n.
The function or position someone has in a team or project.

Daily: What role does each person play in your family's daily routine?

Meeting: Let's define roles and responsibilities before the project kicks off.

responsibility /rɪˌspɒnsɪˈbɪlɪti/ n.
The duty to deal with something; being accountable.

Daily: Owning a pet comes with great responsibility.

Meeting: Ultimate responsibility for this project rests with the project lead.

deliverable /dɪˈlɪvərəbəl/ n.
A product or output to be completed and handed over.

Daily: She tracks her personal deliverables on a simple whiteboard.

Meeting: All deliverables for phase one must be submitted by the 30th.

scope /skoʊp/ n.
The extent or range of something; what is included in a project.

Daily: The scope of the renovation grew as we found more problems.

Meeting: Any additions to the project scope must go through a formal change request.

phase /feɪz/ n.
A distinct stage in a process or cycle.

Daily: We're in the renovation phase of our new home.

Meeting: Phase one focuses on data migration; phase two covers the UI redesign.

framework /ˈfreɪmwɜːrk/ n.
A basic structure or system used as a guide.

Daily: I use a simple personal development framework to set goals each year.

Meeting: We'll use the Agile framework to manage this project from start to finish.

process /ˈproʊses/ n.
A series of steps taken to achieve a result.

Daily: Brewing a perfect cup of pour-over coffee is a satisfying process.

Meeting: The current approval process is too slow and needs to be redesigned.

workflow /ˈwɜːrkfloʊ/ n.
The sequence of steps followed to complete a task.

Daily: I've optimized my home workflow so mornings are less stressful.

Meeting: The new CRM system will automate large parts of the sales workflow.

template /ˈtempleɪt/ n.
A preset format used as a starting point.

Daily: I use a weekly planning template in my journal every Sunday.

Meeting: Please use the standard report template when submitting your findings.

guideline /ˈɡaɪdlaɪn/ n.
A general rule or piece of advice on how to act.

Daily: The travel guidelines suggest packing light for carry-on only.

Meeting: All communications must follow the brand communication guidelines.

protocol /ˈproʊtəkɒl/ n.
A system of rules governing behavior or procedures.

Daily: The hospital has a strict protocol for admitting emergency patients.

Meeting: We need a clear escalation protocol for critical production issues.

procedure /prəˈsiːdʒər/ n.
An established or official way of doing something.

Daily: The procedure for returning items is simple and quick.

Meeting: All staff must follow the security procedure when accessing the server room.

review /rɪˈvjuː/ n.
A formal assessment; a critical appraisal.

Daily: I always read a few reviews before trying a new restaurant.

Meeting: The annual performance review cycle begins in the first week of January.

summary /ˈsʌməri/ n.
A brief statement of the main points.

Daily: Could you give me a summary of the film's plot?

Meeting: Please include a one-page executive summary at the front of the report.

conclusion /kənˈkluːʒən/ n.
The end or final part; a decision reached after analysis.

Daily: The conclusion of the novel was completely unexpected.

Meeting: In conclusion, the data supports the case for a new pricing strategy.

follow-up /ˈfɒloʊ ʌp/ n.
An action taken after a previous one to check or develop it.

Daily: A quick follow-up call showed the customer was very happy.

Meeting: I'll send a follow-up email to confirm the actions from today's meeting.

status /ˈsteɪtəs/ n.
The current condition or situation of something.

Daily: What's the status of your visa application?

Meeting: Please provide a status update by end of day today.

update /ˈʌpdeɪt/ n.
New information about something; a revised version.

Daily: I received a reassuring update from the hospital this morning.

Meeting: The weekly status update should be shared every Friday before noon.

report /rɪˈpɔːrt/ n.
A formal account of the results of an investigation.

Daily: I read a fascinating report on urban planning last weekend.

Meeting: The quarterly report will be presented to the board next Wednesday.

brief /briːf/ n.
A short but comprehensive summary of instructions or facts.

Daily: The design brief for the bedroom was simple: calm and minimal.

Meeting: Please share the creative brief before we bring in the agency team.

overview /ˈoʊvərvjuː/ n.
A general review or summary of a subject.

Daily: The tour guide gave us a brief but fascinating overview of the city's history.

Meeting: I'll start with a two-minute overview of where we are on the project.

background /ˈbækɡraʊnd/ n.
A person's experience; the circumstances leading to an event.

Daily: Tell me a little about your background and what brought you here.

Meeting: Before we dive in, let me give some background on why this project exists.

context /ˈkɒntekst/ n.
The circumstances that form the setting for an event.

Daily: It's important to understand the context before drawing conclusions.

Meeting: To give context: this customer has been with us for over five years.

objective /əbˈdʒektɪv/ n.
A goal or desired result.

Daily: My personal objective for the year is to read 24 books.

Meeting: Let's begin by aligning on the objectives for this project.

action item /ˈækʃən ˈaɪtəm/ n.
A specific task assigned to someone in a meeting.

Daily: I keep a list of personal action items on my fridge.

Meeting: Please capture all action items from today's discussion in the shared doc.

blocker /ˈblɒkər/ n.
Something that prevents progress from being made.

Daily: The only blocker to renovating is finding a reliable contractor.

Meeting: Are there any blockers preventing the team from completing this sprint?

escalation /ˌeskəˈleɪʃən/ n.
The act of raising an issue to a higher authority.

Daily: An escalation of the argument was avoided by a calm mediator.

Meeting: Any issue unresolved within 48 hours should be flagged as an escalation.

sign-off /ˈsaɪn ɒf/ n.
Formal approval or acceptance of something.

Daily: I need sign-off from my co-author before publishing the article.

Meeting: We cannot begin development without written sign-off from the product owner.
