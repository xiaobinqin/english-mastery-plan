Day 1 Core Verbs I — Fundamental Action Words Words 1–50 · 50 words
be /biː/ v.
To exist, or to have a particular quality or state.

Daily: I want to be more organized in my daily life.

Meeting: We need to be fully prepared before the client presentation.

have /hæv/ v.
To possess, own, or experience something.

Daily: I have a coffee every morning before heading out.

Meeting: Do we have all the resources needed for this project?

do /duː/ v.
To perform or carry out an action or task.

Daily: I need to do the laundry before the weekend.

Meeting: What do we need to do to meet the quarterly targets?

say /seɪ/ v.
To speak words aloud; to express something in words.

Daily: She said goodbye before leaving the house.

Meeting: I want to say that everyone's contribution has been truly appreciated.

get /ɡet/ v.
To receive, obtain, or come to be in a particular state.

Daily: I need to get some groceries on the way home.

Meeting: We need to get approval from the stakeholders before moving forward.

make /meɪk/ v.
To create, produce, or cause something to happen.

Daily: Let me make dinner for us tonight.

Meeting: We need to make a final decision by end of day.

go /ɡoʊ/ v.
To move or travel from one place to another.

Daily: I go to the gym every morning before work.

Meeting: The new platform will go live next Monday.

know /noʊ/ v.
To be aware of something or have information about it.

Daily: I know a great restaurant near the park.

Meeting: Does everyone know their role for the upcoming launch?

take /teɪk/ v.
To hold or carry something; to need a certain amount of time.

Daily: I take the subway to work every day.

Meeting: This task will take approximately two weeks to complete.

come /kʌm/ v.
To move toward a place or person; to arrive.

Daily: Come over for dinner on Saturday!

Meeting: Has the client feedback come in yet?

think /θɪŋk/ v.
To use the mind to consider, believe, or form opinions.

Daily: I think I left my keys on the kitchen table.

Meeting: I think we should revisit our current approach to this challenge.

see /siː/ v.
To notice with the eyes; to understand or perceive.

Daily: I can see the mountains clearly from my bedroom window.

Meeting: I see your point, but we must consider the budget implications.

look /lʊk/ v.
To direct your eyes toward something; to appear a certain way.

Daily: Look at the beautiful sunset over the hills!

Meeting: Let's look at last quarter's data before making a decision.

want /wɒnt/ v.
To desire or wish for something.

Daily: I want a hot cup of coffee before I start work.

Meeting: We want to ensure all team members are fully aligned on the objectives.

give /ɡɪv/ v.
To provide or hand over something to someone.

Daily: Can you give me a hand moving this furniture?

Meeting: I'd like to give everyone a brief update on where we stand.

use /juːz/ v.
To employ something for a specific purpose.

Daily: I use my phone to listen to podcasts during my commute.

Meeting: We can use this framework to guide our decision-making process.

find /faɪnd/ v.
To discover or locate something that was missing or unknown.

Daily: I can't find my glasses anywhere!

Meeting: We need to find a practical solution to this bottleneck.

tell /tel/ v.
To communicate information to someone in words.

Daily: Tell me what happened at the party last night!

Meeting: I'll tell you more about the new policy during today's all-hands meeting.

ask /æsk/ v.
To request information, help, or permission.

Daily: I need to ask my neighbor to water my plants.

Meeting: I'd like to ask for your feedback on this proposal.

seem /siːm/ v.
To give the impression of being a certain way.

Daily: She seems a little tired today.

Meeting: The timeline seems tight, but I believe we can manage it.

feel /fiːl/ v.
To experience an emotion or physical sensation.

Daily: I feel much better after a good night's sleep.

Meeting: I feel we've made significant progress this sprint.

try /traɪ/ v.
To make an effort to do something; to test or sample.

Daily: You should try the new Thai restaurant downtown!

Meeting: Let's try a different approach to improve customer retention.

leave /liːv/ v.
To go away from a place; to allow something to stay.

Daily: I leave for work at 8 AM every day.

Meeting: Let's not leave any action items without a clear owner.

call /kɔːl/ v.
To contact by phone; to give a name; to request attendance.

Daily: I'll call you when I'm on my way home.

Meeting: Can we call a quick sync to discuss this issue?

keep /kiːp/ v.
To continue to have or hold something; to maintain a state.

Daily: I keep a daily journal to track my thoughts.

Meeting: Let's keep this discussion focused on our key priorities.

let /let/ v.
To allow or permit someone to do something.

Daily: Let the dog out for a walk after dinner.

Meeting: Let me know if you have any concerns about the timeline.

begin /bɪˈɡɪn/ v.
To start doing or being something.

Daily: I begin each morning with a ten-minute meditation.

Meeting: Let's begin today's meeting with a quick round of progress updates.

show /ʃoʊ/ v.
To display or make something visible; to demonstrate.

Daily: Show me what you painted in art class!

Meeting: I'll show you the quarterly sales figures on the next slide.

hear /hɪr/ v.
To receive sounds through the ears; to be informed of something.

Daily: Can you hear the birds singing outside this morning?

Meeting: I hear there's a new remote-work policy — can you confirm the details?

play /pleɪ/ v.
To engage in activity for enjoyment; to perform a role.

Daily: The kids play in the park every afternoon.

Meeting: Every team member plays a critical role in this project's success.

run /rʌn/ v.
To move quickly on foot; to operate or be in charge of.

Daily: I run five kilometers every morning before breakfast.

Meeting: She runs the marketing department with great efficiency.

move /muːv/ v.
To change position; to cause something to change location.

Daily: We're planning to move to a bigger apartment next month.

Meeting: We need to move this task to the top of our priority list.

live /lɪv/ v.
To be alive; to reside in a particular place.

Daily: I live about ten minutes from the office by bike.

Meeting: We live in an era where rapid change is the norm.

believe /bɪˈliːv/ v.
To think something is true or real; to have confidence in.

Daily: I believe in getting eight hours of sleep every night.

Meeting: I believe this strategy will significantly improve our market position.

hold /hoʊld/ v.
To keep something in your hands; to contain; to organize an event.

Daily: Can you hold this bag for just a moment?

Meeting: We'll hold the kickoff meeting at 10 AM in Conference Room B.

bring /brɪŋ/ v.
To carry or take something to a place.

Daily: Don't forget to bring an umbrella — it looks like rain.

Meeting: Please bring all relevant data and reports to the next planning session.

happen /ˈhæpən/ v.
To occur or take place, often without being planned.

Daily: What happened to you? You look exhausted.

Meeting: What happens if we miss the product launch deadline?

write /raɪt/ v.
To form words or text; to compose a document.

Daily: I write in my journal every night before bed.

Meeting: Please write a brief summary of the meeting outcomes and send it out.

provide /prəˈvaɪd/ v.
To supply or make something available for use.

Daily: This café provides free Wi-Fi for all customers.

Meeting: We need to provide clear guidelines for the new onboarding process.

sit /sɪt/ v.
To rest with your body supported on your lower half.

Daily: I like to sit by the window and read on weekends.

Meeting: Please sit down and we'll get started in just a moment.

stand /stænd/ v.
To be upright on your feet; to represent; to tolerate.

Daily: I stand in line at the coffee shop every morning.

Meeting: Where does our company stand on this important ethical question?

lose /luːz/ v.
To no longer have something; to fail to win.

Daily: I always seem to lose my keys in the morning rush.

Meeting: We cannot afford to lose any more market share to our competitors.

pay /peɪ/ v.
To give money in exchange for goods or services; to give attention.

Daily: I pay most of my bills online these days.

Meeting: We need to pay close attention to the customer feedback data.

meet /miːt/ v.
To come together with someone; to fulfill a requirement.

Daily: Let's meet for coffee on Thursday afternoon.

Meeting: We absolutely need to meet the Friday deadline, no exceptions.

include /ɪnˈkluːd/ v.
To contain something as a part; to add someone or something.

Daily: The hotel rate includes breakfast and free parking.

Meeting: Please include the risk assessment section in your final report.

continue /kənˈtɪnjuː/ v.
To go on doing something without stopping.

Daily: Continue reading the book and we'll discuss it later.

Meeting: Let's continue this discussion after the short break.

set /set/ v.
To put in a position; to establish or fix.

Daily: Set your alarm for 6 AM if you want to catch the early train.

Meeting: We need to set clear expectations at the very beginning of the project.

learn /lɜːrn/ v.
To gain knowledge or skill through study or experience.

Daily: I'm learning Spanish in my spare time using an app.

Meeting: We should learn from our mistakes and adjust our strategy accordingly.

change /tʃeɪndʒ/ v.
To become different; to make something different.

Daily: I need to change my clothes before going out to dinner.

Meeting: We need to change our go-to-market strategy in response to new market data.

lead /liːd/ v.
To guide or direct others; to be first or in charge.

Daily: She leads a popular yoga class every Sunday morning.

Meeting: Who will lead the project during the organizational transition?
