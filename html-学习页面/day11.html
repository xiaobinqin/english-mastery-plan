Day 11 Business & Market Nouns Words 501–549 · 49 words
market /ˈmɑːrkɪt/ n.
The trade in a particular product; a group of buyers.

Daily: The farmer's market every Saturday is one of my favorite routines.

Meeting: We are the clear leader in the enterprise software market.

industry /ˈɪndəstri/ n.
The businesses and manufacturing activity in a sector.

Daily: She has worked in the hospitality industry for over fifteen years.

Meeting: Disruption is happening across every major industry right now.

sector /ˈsektər/ n.
A distinct part of an economy, society, or organization.

Daily: The tech sector offers great opportunities for recent graduates.

Meeting: This regulation impacts all companies in the financial services sector.

organization /ˌɔːrɡənɪˈzeɪʃən/ n.
A group of people with a shared purpose.

Daily: She volunteers for a wonderful organization that supports homeless youth.

Meeting: The entire organization needs to be aligned before this initiative launches.

department /dɪˈpɑːrtmənt/ n.
A distinct section within a company or government.

Daily: He moved to the sales department after years in engineering.

Meeting: Each department head will present their quarterly results next Thursday.

division /dɪˈvɪʒən/ n.
A major section of a large organization.

Daily: The Asia-Pacific division has seen the strongest growth this year.

Meeting: The consumer products division will lead the launch in domestic markets.

staff /stæf/ n.
The people who work for an organization.

Daily: The restaurant staff were incredibly warm and attentive.

Meeting: All staff must complete the cybersecurity training by end of month.

employee /ɪmˈplɔɪiː/ n.
A person who works for an organization in exchange for pay.

Daily: He's been a loyal employee of that company for twenty years.

Meeting: Employee satisfaction scores have improved significantly since last year.

manager /ˈmænɪdʒər/ n.
A person responsible for controlling or directing a team.

Daily: Her manager gave her excellent feedback on the recent presentation.

Meeting: All managers are expected to complete mid-year reviews by next Friday.

director /dɪˈrektər/ n.
A person in charge of a major area of an organization.

Daily: The artistic director completely transformed the festival's program.

Meeting: The board of directors will vote on the acquisition proposal this week.

executive /ɪɡˈzekjutɪv/ n.
A senior manager with decision-making authority.

Daily: She became a C-suite executive before the age of 40.

Meeting: The executive team has approved the new strategic direction.

client /ˈklaɪənt/ n.
A person or organization that uses the services of a business.

Daily: She built strong relationships with each client over many years.

Meeting: The client has requested a demo of the new platform next Tuesday.

customer /ˈkʌstəmər/ n.
A person who buys goods or services from a business.

Daily: The customer experience at that store is second to none.

Meeting: Our goal is to reduce the time it takes to resolve a customer issue.

supplier /səˈplaɪər/ n.
A company or person who provides goods or services.

Daily: We switched to a local supplier for all our fresh produce.

Meeting: We need to qualify a backup supplier to reduce supply chain risk.

partner /ˈpɑːrtnər/ n.
A person or organization sharing in an activity or business.

Daily: She and her business partner launched the startup two years ago.

Meeting: We are excited to announce a new strategic partner in the Southeast Asian market.

competitor /kəmˈpetɪtər/ n.
A person or company that competes against another.

Daily: Their main competitor released a very similar product last year.

Meeting: We need to monitor what our top three competitors are doing in this space.

investor /ɪnˈvestər/ n.
A person or organization that invests money.

Daily: Angel investors helped fund her startup in its early stages.

Meeting: Investor confidence in our company is at an all-time high.

stakeholder /ˈsteɪkhoʊldər/ n.
A person with an interest in the outcome of a project.

Daily: All key stakeholders were invited to the community consultation.

Meeting: We must keep all stakeholders informed at every stage of the project.

budget /ˈbʌdʒɪt/ n.
An estimate of income and expenditure; an allocated amount.

Daily: I made a strict monthly budget to save for our holiday.

Meeting: The project budget has been approved at $1.2 million.

revenue /ˈrevənjuː/ n.
Income generated by a business.

Daily: She earns additional revenue from her online tutoring services.

Meeting: Total revenue for Q3 was 12% above our initial forecast.

profit /ˈprɒfɪt/ n.
Financial gain after deducting costs from revenue.

Daily: The small café turned a modest profit in its first year.

Meeting: Our net profit margin has improved from 8% to 11% this year.

expense /ɪkˈspens/ n.
The cost required for something; money spent.

Daily: Childcare is the biggest monthly expense for our family.

Meeting: All business expenses must be submitted with receipts for reimbursement.

investment /ɪnˈvestmənt/ n.
Money or resources committed for future benefit.

Daily: Buying this apartment was the best investment I ever made.

Meeting: This is a strategic investment that will pay dividends over three years.

capital /ˈkæpɪtəl/ n.
Money used for investment; the main city of a country.

Daily: She raised enough capital to open her first restaurant.

Meeting: We are seeking additional capital to fund the next phase of expansion.

asset /ˈæset/ n.
Something valuable owned by a person or company.

Daily: Loyalty is the most valuable asset you can build in a relationship.

Meeting: Our proprietary data is our most valuable competitive asset.

contract /ˈkɒntrækt/ n.
A formal agreement between two or more parties.

Daily: She read the entire rental contract before signing.

Meeting: The contract has been reviewed by legal and is ready for signature.

agreement /əˈɡriːmənt/ n.
An arrangement made between two or more parties.

Daily: We reached an agreement on where to go for the holidays.

Meeting: We need a written agreement in place before work begins.

deal /diːl/ n.
An agreement or arrangement; a transaction.

Daily: That restaurant offers a brilliant lunch deal on weekdays.

Meeting: We are close to closing a major deal with a global enterprise client.

proposal /prəˈpoʊzəl/ n.
A formal suggestion or plan put forward for consideration.

Daily: She submitted a proposal to open a café in the community center.

Meeting: The proposal clearly outlines the scope, timeline, and estimated costs.

strategy /ˈstrætɪdʒi/ n.
A plan of action to achieve a long-term goal.

Daily: Her strategy for the chess tournament was methodical and brilliant.

Meeting: Our go-to-market strategy needs to be finalized before the product launch.

objective /əbˈdʒektɪv/ n.
A goal or aim that one is working toward.

Daily: My main objective this year is to learn a new language.

Meeting: Each team member should have two or three measurable objectives for Q3.

target /ˈtɑːrɡɪt/ n.
A goal or desired result; a person or thing aimed at.

Daily: My running target this month is a total of 80 kilometers.

Meeting: We hit every revenue target for the second consecutive quarter.

performance /pərˈfɔːrməns/ n.
How well someone or something functions; an act or show.

Daily: The team's performance at the tournament was truly impressive.

Meeting: Individual performance will be assessed against agreed-upon KPIs.

growth /ɡroʊθ/ n.
The process of increasing or developing.

Daily: Personal growth requires stepping outside your comfort zone.

Meeting: We are targeting 25% year-on-year revenue growth across all regions.

expansion /ɪkˈspænʃən/ n.
The process of becoming larger in size or scope.

Daily: The company's global expansion has created hundreds of new jobs.

Meeting: International expansion is our top strategic priority for the next two years.

competition /ˌkɒmpɪˈtɪʃən/ n.
The activity of competing; the people competing.

Daily: The baking competition at the school fair was fiercely contested.

Meeting: Intensifying competition means we need to innovate faster than ever.

regulation /ˌreɡjuˈleɪʃən/ n.
A rule made by an authority to control something.

Daily: There are strict regulations on food labeling in this country.

Meeting: We must ensure full compliance with the new data protection regulation.

compliance /kəmˈplaɪəns/ n.
The act of following rules, laws, or standards.

Daily: Compliance with health and safety guidelines is everyone's responsibility.

Meeting: The compliance audit found no major issues with our current processes.

operation /ˌɒpəˈreɪʃən/ n.
An activity or business carried out; how something functions.

Daily: The rescue operation lasted over 18 hours and ended successfully.

Meeting: Day-to-day operations are running smoothly across all three offices.

quality /ˈkwɒlɪti/ n.
The standard of something; a good characteristic.

Daily: Quality time with family is more important than quantity.

Meeting: We never compromise on quality — that is the foundation of our brand.

standard /ˈstændərd/ n.
A level of quality; an accepted norm or guideline.

Daily: She holds herself to very high personal standards.

Meeting: All deliverables must meet the standards outlined in the project charter.

benchmark /ˈbentʃmɑːrk/ n.
A standard used for comparison or reference.

Daily: That product has become the benchmark for quality in the industry.

Meeting: We'll benchmark our performance against industry leaders for context.

criteria /kraɪˈtɪərɪə/ n.
Standards or principles used to judge or decide something.

Daily: The main criteria for choosing a school were location and class size.

Meeting: What are the evaluation criteria for selecting the winning proposal?

evaluation /ɪˌvæljuˈeɪʃən/ n.
The making of a judgement about quality or performance.

Daily: The evaluation of the new recipe took three full days.

Meeting: The final vendor evaluation will be completed by end of next week.

assessment /əˈsesmənt/ n.
The process of evaluating or judging something.

Daily: A professional skills assessment helped her identify career gaps.

Meeting: A risk assessment must be completed before the project kicks off.

recommendation /ˌrekəmenˈdeɪʃən/ n.
A suggestion that something is good or suitable.

Daily: The doctor's recommendation was to rest completely for two weeks.

Meeting: Our recommendation is to adopt a phased rollout rather than a big bang.

implementation /ˌɪmplɪmenˈteɪʃən/ n.
The process of putting a plan into action.

Daily: The implementation of the new gym routine was easier than expected.

Meeting: Successful implementation requires buy-in from all key stakeholders.

measurement /ˈmeʒərmənt/ n.
The act of measuring; a figure showing size or amount.

Daily: I needed exact measurements before ordering the custom curtains.

Meeting: Clear measurement criteria are essential for evaluating success.

improvement /ɪmˈpruːvmənt/ n.
The act of making something better; a change for the better.

Daily: I've noticed a real improvement in my stamina since I started swimming.

Meeting: Continuous improvement is a core value of our engineering culture.
