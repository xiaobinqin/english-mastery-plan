Day 5 Core Adjectives I — Describing the World Words 201–250 · 50 words
good /ɡʊd/ adj.
Of high quality; pleasant; skilled; morally right.

Daily: This is the best cup of coffee I've had in a while.

Meeting: The team has done a good job managing expectations with the client.

new /njuː/ adj.
Recently made, produced, or arrived; not existing before.

Daily: I bought a new pair of running shoes for the marathon.

Meeting: Let me introduce a new process for handling customer escalations.

old /oʊld/ adj.
Having existed for a long time; of a particular age.

Daily: I reconnected with an old friend from university last week.

Meeting: Some of our old workflows are no longer efficient — it's time to update them.

great /ɡreɪt/ adj.
Very large in size or amount; very good or impressive.

Daily: We had a great time at the outdoor concert on Saturday.

Meeting: This is a great opportunity to expand into the enterprise market.

own /oʊn/ adj.
Belonging to a particular person.

Daily: I dream of owning my own home one day.

Meeting: Each team lead is responsible for managing their own department budget.

other /ˈʌðər/ adj.
Different from the one already mentioned; additional.

Daily: On the other hand, I also enjoy quiet evenings at home.

Meeting: Are there other options we haven't considered yet?

right /raɪt/ adj.
Correct; most suitable; on the side opposite to left.

Daily: Make sure you take the right turn at the roundabout.

Meeting: We need to make sure we're asking the right questions first.

large /lɑːrdʒ/ adj.
Big in size, number, or amount.

Daily: We need a large table for the family dinner this Sunday.

Meeting: We are serving a large and growing enterprise customer segment.

high /haɪ/ adj.
Far above the ground; great in amount or level.

Daily: House prices in this area are extremely high.

Meeting: We need to maintain high standards throughout the entire project.

small /smɔːl/ adj.
Little in size, number, or amount.

Daily: The apartment is small but perfectly organized.

Meeting: Let's start with a small pilot before rolling out to all users.

long /lɒŋ/ adj.
Measuring a great distance from end to end; lasting a long time.

Daily: It's been a long day — I'm ready for bed!

Meeting: This is a long-term investment that will pay off over time.

big /bɪɡ/ adj.
Large in size or importance; great in degree.

Daily: We're planning a big family celebration for my parents' anniversary.

Meeting: This is a big decision that will affect the entire organization.

important /ɪmˈpɔːrtənt/ adj.
Of great significance or value.

Daily: Getting enough sleep is incredibly important for your health.

Meeting: The most important thing is that we deliver quality, not just speed.

public /ˈpʌblɪk/ adj.
Relating to or involving all people in a community.

Daily: The park is open to the public every day from dawn to dusk.

Meeting: We cannot share this information until there is a public announcement.

real /riːl/ adj.
Actually existing, not imagined or pretend; genuine.

Daily: The real reason I was late was the traffic jam on the motorway.

Meeting: We need to address the real root cause, not just the symptoms.

best /best/ adj.
Of the highest quality, most excellent.

Daily: This is the best pizza I've ever had in my life.

Meeting: What's the best approach to solving this particular challenge?

free /friː/ adj.
Not costing anything; not restricted or controlled.

Daily: I love exploring the city on free Sunday afternoons.

Meeting: Feel free to reach out if you have any questions after the meeting.

able /ˈeɪbəl/ adj.
Having the skill or means to do something.

Daily: I'm finally able to run 5km without stopping.

Meeting: Are we able to deliver this by the end of next week?

early /ˈɜːrli/ adj.
Happening near the beginning of a period; before the expected time.

Daily: I prefer early morning flights to avoid the airport rush.

Meeting: We're making an early decision to pivot before we invest further.

late /leɪt/ adj.
After the expected or usual time; near the end of a period.

Daily: I had a late lunch and now I'm not hungry for dinner.

Meeting: We are already two weeks late and need to accelerate delivery.

hard /hɑːrd/ adj.
Requiring a lot of effort; firm and solid to the touch.

Daily: Learning a new language is hard but so rewarding.

Meeting: This has been a hard year, but the team has really stepped up.

clear /klɪr/ adj.
Easy to understand; transparent; free from obstruction.

Daily: The instructions were clear and easy to follow.

Meeting: Please make sure the requirements are clear before development starts.

recent /ˈriːsənt/ adj.
Happening or appearing not long ago.

Daily: Have you seen his most recent exhibition at the art gallery?

Meeting: Based on recent customer feedback, we need to improve the onboarding flow.

young /jʌŋ/ adj.
In an early stage of life; having little experience.

Daily: She looks incredibly young for her age.

Meeting: We're building a team of young, high-potential talent.

national /ˈnæʃənəl/ adj.
Relating to or belonging to a nation as a whole.

Daily: The national holiday next week means the office will be closed.

Meeting: We are launching a national marketing campaign in Q3.

open /ˈoʊpən/ adj.
Not closed; available for use; honest and communicative.

Daily: The restaurant is open from 8 AM to 10 PM.

Meeting: I encourage everyone to be open and honest in this retrospective.

full /fʊl/ adj.
Containing as much as possible; complete; thorough.

Daily: I'm absolutely full after that three-course dinner.

Meeting: Please provide a full breakdown of costs in the financial section.

possible /ˈpɒsɪbəl/ adj.
Able to happen or be achieved; that may or may not be true.

Daily: Is it possible to get a table for four at short notice?

Meeting: Is it possible to deliver the first version by end of this month?

simple /ˈsɪmpəl/ adj.
Easy to understand or do; not complex or complicated.

Daily: The recipe is really simple — anyone can make it.

Meeting: Let's keep the message simple so all stakeholders can understand it.

available /əˈveɪləbəl/ adj.
Present and ready to be used; free to be met.

Daily: The book is available at your local library for free.

Meeting: Are you available for a call on Thursday morning?

local /ˈloʊkəl/ adj.
Belonging to or relating to a particular area.

Daily: I always prefer to shop at the local market.

Meeting: We need to comply with local regulations in each country we operate.

social /ˈsoʊʃəl/ adj.
Relating to society or human interaction.

Daily: Social media has completely changed the way we communicate.

Meeting: We should consider the social impact of this product feature.

major /ˈmeɪdʒər/ adj.
Very large, important, or serious; main or chief.

Daily: Moving to a new city was a major change in my life.

Meeting: This is a major milestone for the entire company.

main /meɪn/ adj.
Most important; principal; chief.

Daily: The main reason I love this city is the food culture.

Meeting: The main objective of today's meeting is to align on the roadmap.

true /truː/ adj.
In accordance with fact or reality; genuine.

Daily: Is it true that you're moving to a new country?

Meeting: It's true that we are behind schedule, but we have a recovery plan.

current /ˈkɜːrənt/ adj.
Belonging to or happening at the present time.

Daily: The current state of the kitchen is a bit chaotic!

Meeting: What is the current status of the project?

strong /strɒŋ/ adj.
Having great power; firm; intense.

Daily: She is one of the strongest people I've ever met.

Meeting: We have a strong competitive advantage in this market segment.

different /ˈdɪfərənt/ adj.
Not the same as another; distinct.

Daily: We tried a different route today and discovered a beautiful park.

Meeting: I'd like to consider a different approach to this problem.

personal /ˈpɜːrsənəl/ adj.
Relating to a specific person; private.

Daily: I prefer to keep my personal and professional lives separate.

Meeting: This is a personal matter and should not be discussed at work.

special /ˈspeʃəl/ adj.
Different from the ordinary; deserving particular attention.

Daily: We booked a special dinner to celebrate our anniversary.

Meeting: There is a special section in the report dedicated to risk analysis.

human /ˈhjuːmən/ adj.
Relating to or characteristic of people.

Daily: It's only human to make mistakes sometimes.

Meeting: We must consider the human impact of any organizational changes.

various /ˈveərɪəs/ adj.
Of different kinds; more than one; several.

Daily: I enjoy cooking various cuisines from around the world.

Meeting: There are various options on the table — let's evaluate each one.

dark /dɑːrk/ adj.
With little or no light; gloomy; of deep color.

Daily: I like to sleep in a completely dark room.

Meeting: Let's not ignore the dark side of this proposal — there are real risks.

light /laɪt/ adj.
Not heavy; bright; pale in color.

Daily: I prefer light meals in the morning.

Meeting: We need a light-touch approach to avoid disrupting current workflows.

next /nekst/ adj.
Coming immediately after; nearest in position.

Daily: I'm really looking forward to next weekend's camping trip.

Meeting: The next step is to get stakeholder sign-off on the updated design.

last /lɑːst/ adj.
Coming after all others; most recent.

Daily: Last night's dinner was absolutely delicious.

Meeting: As I mentioned in last week's update, we are on track.

total /ˈtoʊtəl/ adj.
Including everything; complete and absolute.

Daily: The total bill came to more than we expected.

Meeting: The total budget for this initiative is $2.4 million.

political /pəˈlɪtɪkəl/ adj.
Relating to government, politics, or public affairs.

Daily: I try not to discuss political views at family dinners.

Meeting: We need to be aware of the political context in each market we enter.

economic /ˌiːkəˈnɒmɪk/ adj.
Relating to money, trade, and industry.

Daily: The economic impact of the pandemic was felt globally.

Meeting: Let's review the economic projections for the next fiscal year.

general /ˈdʒenərəl/ adj.
Affecting or relating to most people or things; not specific.

Daily: The general feeling at the party was warm and celebratory.

Meeting: I'd like to start with some general observations before diving into specifics.
