Day 9 Communication Verbs — Talking & Listening Words 401–448 · 48 words
announce /əˈnaʊns/ v.
To make something known publicly or formally.

Daily: The couple announced their engagement at the family dinner.

Meeting: I'm pleased to announce that we've signed a major new partnership.

explain /ɪkˈspleɪn/ v.
To make something clear by describing it in detail.

Daily: Could you explain how to use this new coffee machine?

Meeting: I'll explain the rationale behind the new workflow on the next slide.

describe /dɪˈskraɪb/ v.
To give an account of what something is like.

Daily: Can you describe your ideal weekend to me?

Meeting: Please describe your current process in as much detail as possible.

introduce /ˌɪntrəˈdjuːs/ v.
To present someone or something for the first time.

Daily: Let me introduce you to my neighbor and dear friend, Maria.

Meeting: I'd like to take a moment to introduce our new project sponsor.

clarify /ˈklærɪfaɪ/ v.
To make something clearer and easier to understand.

Daily: Can you clarify what you meant by that comment?

Meeting: I'd like to clarify the scope of work before we discuss pricing.

request /rɪˈkwest/ v.
To politely ask for something formally.

Daily: I requested a window seat when I checked in online.

Meeting: We formally request an extension of two weeks on the submission deadline.

respond /rɪˈspɒnd/ v.
To say or do something in reaction to something.

Daily: She responded to my message almost immediately.

Meeting: Please respond to all client emails within one business day.

reply /rɪˈplaɪ/ v.
To give an answer or response to someone.

Daily: I forgot to reply to his text message — I'll do it now.

Meeting: I'll reply to the client's questions by end of business today.

inform /ɪnˈfɔːrm/ v.
To give someone facts or information.

Daily: Please inform me when your flight lands.

Meeting: I wanted to inform you of a change to today's agenda.

notify /ˈnoʊtɪfaɪ/ v.
To formally tell someone about something.

Daily: The app will notify you when the order has been dispatched.

Meeting: Please notify all stakeholders of the new release date by tomorrow.

advise /ədˈvaɪz/ v.
To give recommendations or suggestions to someone.

Daily: My doctor advised me to cut back on caffeine.

Meeting: I would advise caution before committing to this timeline.

remind /rɪˈmaɪnd/ v.
To cause someone to remember something.

Daily: Remind me to buy milk before we get home.

Meeting: Just a reminder that the quarterly review is scheduled for Thursday.

thank /θæŋk/ v.
To express gratitude to someone.

Daily: I wrote a note to thank my neighbor for feeding my cat.

Meeting: I'd like to thank each one of you for your commitment this quarter.

welcome /ˈwelkəm/ v.
To greet someone in a friendly way; to receive gladly.

Daily: We always welcome guests with a home-cooked meal.

Meeting: I'd like to welcome our new colleague, James, who joins us from Singapore.

invite /ɪnˈvaɪt/ v.
To ask someone to come or participate.

Daily: I was invited to speak at the local community festival.

Meeting: I'd like to invite the legal team to join us for the next discussion.

apologize /əˈpɒlədʒaɪz/ v.
To say you are sorry for something.

Daily: I apologized to my friend for forgetting her birthday.

Meeting: I apologize for the confusion caused by the earlier email.

complain /kəmˈpleɪn/ v.
To express dissatisfaction or unhappiness.

Daily: He always complains about the weather but never moves!

Meeting: A client has complained about the slow response time on tickets.

argue /ˈɑːrɡjuː/ v.
To give reasons for or against; to disagree with someone.

Daily: My roommates argued for an hour about which restaurant to choose.

Meeting: I would argue that the root cause is actually a process issue, not a people one.

debate /dɪˈbeɪt/ v.
To discuss a topic in a formal way; to consider both sides.

Daily: We debated for hours whether to get a cat or a dog.

Meeting: We've been debating this approach for two sprints — it's time to decide.

challenge /ˈtʃælɪndʒ/ v.
To question or dispute something; to invite competition.

Daily: He challenged himself to read a book every week for a year.

Meeting: I'd like to respectfully challenge the assumptions in this proposal.

defend /dɪˈfend/ v.
To protect from harm or criticism; to argue in favor of.

Daily: A good lawyer always defends their client with full dedication.

Meeting: Are you prepared to defend your budget estimates to the board?

criticize /ˈkrɪtɪsaɪz/ v.
To say what is wrong with something; to judge negatively.

Daily: It's easy to criticize, but much harder to offer real solutions.

Meeting: Please be constructive when you criticize — frame it with a suggestion.

praise /preɪz/ v.
To express strong approval or admiration.

Daily: She praised her team publicly after a challenging project.

Meeting: I want to praise the engineering team for the seamless deployment last week.

encourage /ɪnˈkʌrɪdʒ/ v.
To give confidence or motivation to someone.

Daily: My coach always encourages me to push past my comfort zone.

Meeting: I want to encourage everyone to raise issues early rather than waiting.

persuade /pərˈsweɪd/ v.
To cause someone to believe or do something by reasoning.

Daily: She persuaded her parents to let her study abroad for a year.

Meeting: How can we persuade the client that a phased delivery is in their best interest?

refuse /rɪˈfjuːz/ v.
To say no to something; to decline.

Daily: He refused the dessert, which is very unlike him!

Meeting: We had to refuse the request as it fell outside the agreed scope.

accept /əkˈsept/ v.
To receive or agree to something willingly.

Daily: She accepted the job offer and is starting next month.

Meeting: We are pleased to accept your proposal and look forward to working together.

share /ʃeər/ v.
To give a portion to others; to tell or show something to others.

Daily: Would you like to share a dessert? I can't finish a whole one.

Meeting: I'd like to share some early findings before the full report is ready.

express /ɪkˈspres/ v.
To put thoughts or feelings into words or actions.

Daily: She expressed her gratitude by baking a cake for the whole team.

Meeting: Please feel free to express any concerns openly during this retrospective.

indicate /ˈɪndɪkeɪt/ v.
To show or point out; to be a sign of.

Daily: The weather forecast indicates heavy rain tomorrow morning.

Meeting: The data indicates a clear correlation between response time and churn rate.

state /steɪt/ v.
To say or write something clearly and formally.

Daily: Please state your name and date of birth for the record.

Meeting: As I stated in my earlier email, the deadline is non-negotiable.

note /noʊt/ v.
To observe and remember; to write down as a record.

Daily: Note the beautiful architecture as we walk through the old city.

Meeting: Please note that this decision is subject to board approval.

mention /ˈmenʃən/ v.
To refer to something briefly.

Daily: Did I mention that I'm taking a week off next month?

Meeting: I just want to mention that the budget figures are still provisional.

highlight /ˈhaɪlaɪt/ v.
To draw attention to something important.

Daily: She highlighted the key passages in the textbook with a yellow marker.

Meeting: I want to highlight three wins from this week before we discuss blockers.

emphasize /ˈemfəsaɪz/ v.
To give special importance to something.

Daily: My doctor emphasized the importance of daily physical activity.

Meeting: I want to emphasize that quality must not be compromised to meet the deadline.

demonstrate /ˈdemənstreɪt/ v.
To show clearly; to prove by evidence.

Daily: She demonstrated the new cooking technique step by step.

Meeting: The pilot results clearly demonstrate the value of this new approach.

illustrate /ˈɪləstreɪt/ v.
To make something clear with examples or pictures.

Daily: Let me illustrate my point with a simple analogy.

Meeting: This chart illustrates the trend in customer acquisition cost over time.

summarize /ˈsʌməraɪz/ v.
To give a brief statement of the main points.

Daily: Could you summarize the plot of that movie for me?

Meeting: To summarize: we are on track, but we need two additional developers.

conclude /kənˈkluːd/ v.
To bring to an end; to decide or determine after consideration.

Daily: She concluded her speech with a moving personal story.

Meeting: We can conclude from this data that our new pricing model is working.

distribute /dɪˈstrɪbjuːt/ v.
To hand out or spread to multiple people or places.

Daily: We distributed homemade cookies to all the neighbors at the festival.

Meeting: Please distribute the meeting minutes to all attendees by end of day.

publish /ˈpʌblɪʃ/ v.
To make information available to the public or to a group.

Daily: She published her first novel after years of writing.

Meeting: We'll publish the final results on the company intranet next Monday.

interact /ˌɪntərˈækt/ v.
To communicate and act with others.

Daily: Children learn many social skills through play and interaction.

Meeting: Our chatbot allows customers to interact with our service 24/7.

consult /kənˈsʌlt/ v.
To seek information or advice from someone.

Daily: I always consult a map before exploring a new city on foot.

Meeting: Before deciding, we should consult with both the legal and finance teams.

interview /ˈɪntərvjuː/ v.
To formally question someone for a position or information.

Daily: I'm interviewing for a new role later this week.

Meeting: We'll interview five shortlisted candidates over the next two days.

collaborate /kəˈlæbəreɪt/ v.
To work together with others on a project.

Daily: The two chefs collaborated to create a unique fusion menu.

Meeting: This project requires all three departments to collaborate closely.

engage /ɪnˈɡeɪdʒ/ v.
To attract and hold attention; to involve or participate.

Daily: The speaker engaged the audience with humor and storytelling.

Meeting: We need to better engage our remote team members during virtual meetings.

exchange /ɪksˈtʃeɪndʒ/ v.
To give and receive something in return; to swap.

Daily: We exchanged gifts at the holiday party and everyone loved theirs.

Meeting: Let's exchange contact details so we can follow up after the conference.

promote /prəˈmoʊt/ v.
To support or encourage; to raise to a higher position.

Daily: She was promoted to senior manager after just two years.

Meeting: This campaign will help us promote the new product to a wider audience.
