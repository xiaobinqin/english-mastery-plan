Day 20 Key Abstract & Leadership Nouns Words 951–995 · 45 words
opportunity /ˌɒpəˈtjuːnɪti/ n.
A time or situation that makes something possible.

Daily: Every challenge you face is also an opportunity to grow stronger.

Meeting: This partnership is a significant opportunity to accelerate our growth.

challenge /ˈtʃælɪndʒ/ n.
Something difficult that tests your abilities.

Daily: Learning to cook from scratch was my biggest challenge this year.

Meeting: The main challenge is balancing speed with quality in this tight timeline.

achievement /əˈtʃiːvmənt/ n.
Something accomplished successfully through effort.

Daily: Raising two kids while running a business is her greatest achievement.

Meeting: Completing this project on time and under budget is a major team achievement.

innovation /ˌɪnəˈveɪʃən/ n.
A new idea, method, or device; the process of innovating.

Daily: The innovation in her cooking keeps every dinner party exciting.

Meeting: Continuous innovation is the only sustainable competitive advantage.

strategy /ˈstrætɪdʒi/ n.
A plan designed to achieve a long-term goal.

Daily: Her strategy for learning a language is simple: use it every day.

Meeting: Our three-year strategy is focused on profitability, not just growth.

initiative /ɪˈnɪʃətɪv/ n.
The ability to act independently; a new plan or project.

Daily: She showed great initiative by organizing the neighborhood clean-up.

Meeting: This is a company-wide initiative and requires support from all departments.

priority /praɪˈɒrɪti/ n.
The most important thing; something given special attention.

Daily: Family is and will always be my highest priority.

Meeting: The product roadmap reflects our priorities for the next two quarters.

accountability /əˌkaʊntəˈbɪlɪti/ n.
The fact of being responsible for one's actions.

Daily: Accountability is one of the values I teach my children every day.

Meeting: Clear ownership and accountability are the foundations of a high-performing team.

transparency /trænsˈpærənsi/ n.
The quality of being open and not hiding information.

Daily: She appreciates transparency in any relationship — professional or personal.

Meeting: Transparency with stakeholders builds the trust necessary for partnership.

sustainability /səˌsteɪnəˈbɪlɪti/ n.
The ability to continue without damaging the environment or resources.

Daily: We make purchasing decisions based on sustainability as much as quality.

Meeting: Sustainability is a core pillar of our long-term corporate strategy.

efficiency /ɪˈfɪʃənsi/ n.
The quality of achieving maximum results with minimum waste.

Daily: Good planning dramatically improves daily efficiency.

Meeting: Process efficiency must improve if we are to scale without adding headcount.

productivity /ˌprɒdʌkˈtɪvɪti/ n.
The efficiency with which work or output is produced.

Daily: Deep work sessions with no interruptions maximize my productivity.

Meeting: Team productivity has increased by 25% since adopting the new workflow.

flexibility /ˌfleksɪˈbɪlɪti/ n.
The ability to change or be changed easily; adaptability.

Daily: Flexibility in my schedule allows me to make time for what matters most.

Meeting: We need flexibility in the delivery plan to accommodate changing requirements.

adaptability /əˌdæptəˈbɪlɪti/ n.
The ability to adjust to new conditions.

Daily: Adaptability is the skill that has served me most in life.

Meeting: Adaptability is one of the top qualities we look for in every new hire.

resilience /rɪˈzɪlɪəns/ n.
The ability to recover quickly from difficulties.

Daily: Resilience is built through repeated exposure to discomfort.

Meeting: The team's resilience in the face of setbacks has been truly impressive.

engagement /ɪnˈɡeɪdʒmənt/ n.
Involvement and commitment; interaction with something.

Daily: Her engagement in the community makes her a widely respected figure.

Meeting: Employee engagement scores are at a three-year high after the culture initiative.

motivation /ˌmoʊtɪˈveɪʃən/ n.
The reason for acting in a particular way; drive.

Daily: My biggest motivation in life is watching the people I love succeed.

Meeting: Understanding what drives motivation is key to effective leadership.

satisfaction /ˌsætɪsˈfækʃən/ n.
The feeling of being pleased; fulfillment of a need.

Daily: There's a deep satisfaction in completing a creative project.

Meeting: Customer satisfaction is our most important performance indicator.

capability /ˌkeɪpəˈbɪlɪti/ n.
The power or ability to do something.

Daily: She has the capability to lead a team three times the size of her current one.

Meeting: We need to assess our internal capability before deciding to outsource.

capacity /kəˈpæsɪti/ n.
The maximum amount; the ability to do something.

Daily: My schedule is at full capacity — I need to say no to something.

Meeting: Does the team have the capacity to take on an additional sprint deliverable?

potential /pəˈtenʃəl/ n.
Qualities that may develop into something in the future.

Daily: She has the potential to become one of the best in her field.

Meeting: We're not yet tapping the full potential of our data assets.

limitation /ˌlɪmɪˈteɪʃən/ n.
A restriction; a weakness or flaw.

Daily: Knowing your limitations is a sign of wisdom, not weakness.

Meeting: The main limitation of this approach is the cost at scale.

constraint /kənˈstreɪnt/ n.
A limitation or restriction on what can be done.

Daily: Working within constraints often forces the most creative solutions.

Meeting: Budget, time, and resources are the three core constraints on this project.

assumption /əˈsʌmpʃən/ n.
Something accepted as true without proof.

Daily: I made the assumption it would be warm and didn't pack a coat.

Meeting: Let's document all assumptions in the project charter and validate them early.

perspective /pərˈspektɪv/ n.
A way of looking at things; relative importance.

Daily: Traveling alone completely changed my perspective on my own life.

Meeting: I'd welcome different perspectives from across the team before we decide.

framework /ˈfreɪmwɜːrk/ n.
A structure within which something is organized.

Daily: I use a simple framework to decide whether to say yes or no to requests.

Meeting: The project will be managed using the Agile framework from kickoff.

principle /ˈprɪnsɪpəl/ n.
A fundamental rule or belief guiding behavior.

Daily: She lives by the principle that kindness costs nothing.

Meeting: Every product decision must align with our core design principles.

benchmark /ˈbentʃmɑːrk/ n.
A standard for comparison.

Daily: I use a best-in-class product as a personal benchmark.

Meeting: We'll benchmark our performance against the top three industry players.

criteria /kraɪˈtɪərɪə/ n.
Principles used to judge or evaluate something.

Daily: The selection criteria for the recipe contest were originality and taste.

Meeting: What are the acceptance criteria for this feature to be approved?

implementation /ˌɪmplɪmenˈteɪʃən/ n.
The process of putting a plan into action.

Daily: The implementation of better sleep habits took me a full month.

Meeting: Successful implementation depends on clear ownership and communication.

recommendation /ˌrekəmenˈdeɪʃən/ n.
A suggestion that something is good or suitable.

Daily: Her doctor's recommendation was to rest for a full week.

Meeting: Our recommendation is to run a three-month pilot before full rollout.

outcome /ˈaʊtkʌm/ n.
A result or consequence of a process or action.

Daily: The outcome of the experiment confirmed the hypothesis.

Meeting: The desired outcome is a fully automated reporting process by end of year.

impact /ˈɪmpækt/ n.
A powerful effect on someone or something.

Daily: The impact of her speech was felt by everyone in the room.

Meeting: We need to measure the business impact of this change over 90 days.

influence /ˈɪnfluəns/ n.
The power to affect others; the effect of one thing on another.

Daily: She has been the greatest positive influence in my professional life.

Meeting: Customer voice should be the primary influence on our product roadmap.

contribution /ˌkɒntrɪˈbjuːʃən/ n.
Something given or done to help produce a result.

Daily: Every small contribution matters when you're building something great.

Meeting: I want to acknowledge the extraordinary contribution of every team member.

commitment /kəˈmɪtmənt/ n.
The state of being dedicated to an activity or cause.

Daily: My commitment to daily exercise has changed my life.

Meeting: I appreciate the team's commitment to delivering excellence every day.

dedication /ˌdedɪˈkeɪʃən/ n.
The quality of being committed to a task.

Daily: His dedication to mastering the craft is truly inspiring.

Meeting: The team's dedication is the single most important reason for this success.

professionalism /prəˈfeʃənəlɪzəm/ n.
The skill and behavior expected of a professional.

Daily: She handles every interaction with remarkable professionalism.

Meeting: Professionalism in client communications is a non-negotiable expectation.

excellence /ˈeksələns/ n.
The quality of being outstanding.

Daily: She pursues excellence in everything she takes on.

Meeting: We have a culture of excellence — good enough is never good enough.

quality /ˈkwɒlɪti/ n.
The standard of something; a distinctive characteristic.

Daily: Quality over quantity is my guiding philosophy in everything I do.

Meeting: Quality is the one thing we will never sacrifice to hit a deadline.

leadership /ˈliːdərʃɪp/ n.
The action of leading; the ability to lead.

Daily: Real leadership is about serving others, not directing them.

Meeting: Strong leadership from the project sponsor is critical to this initiative.

vision /ˈvɪʒən/ n.
The ability to plan for the future; a mental image.

Daily: Her vision for the garden was ambitious — and it turned out even better.

Meeting: Our ten-year vision is to be the most trusted platform in our sector.

mission /ˈmɪʃən/ n.
An important goal or task; an organization's purpose.

Daily: My personal mission is to leave every place better than I found it.

Meeting: Our company mission is to make quality healthcare accessible to everyone.

values /ˈvæljuːz/ n.
Important beliefs about what is right and wrong; guiding principles.

Daily: My values around honesty and kindness shape every decision I make.

Meeting: The new hire must be a strong cultural and values fit for the team.

The ideas, behaviors, and customs of a group.

Daily: Creating a family culture of reading starts with modeling the habit yourself.

Meeting: We are actively working to build a culture of psychological safety.
