Day 7 Common Adverbs — How & How Much Words 301–349 · 49 words
also /ˈɔːlsoʊ/ adv.
In addition; too; as well.

Daily: I love hiking and I also enjoy kayaking.

Meeting: I'd also like to address the resource allocation issue before we close.

well /wel/ adv.
In a good way; to a high standard; thoroughly.

Daily: She handles pressure extremely well.

Meeting: The project is going well — we're on schedule and within budget.

just /dʒʌst/ adv.
A very short time ago; exactly; only; simply.

Daily: I just got back from a wonderful trip to Portugal.

Meeting: I just wanted to clarify the timeline before the call ends.

now /naʊ/ adv.
At this moment; immediately; given the current situation.

Daily: I need to leave now or I'll miss my train.

Meeting: We need to make a decision now before the window closes.

still /stɪl/ adv.
Continuing until now or a specified time; even.

Daily: Are you still enjoying that new podcast I recommended?

Meeting: We are still waiting for the legal team's approval on the contract.

even /ˈiːvən/ adv.
Used to emphasize something surprising.

Daily: Even the pickiest eaters loved the meal I prepared.

Meeting: Even with the additional resources, we may still miss the deadline.

then /ðen/ adv.
At that time; after that; therefore; in that case.

Daily: I finished the book, then made a pot of tea.

Meeting: If we agree on the scope today, then we can kick off development next Monday.

actually /ˈæktʃuəli/ adv.
In reality; used to correct or emphasize a fact.

Daily: I actually prefer cooking at home to eating out.

Meeting: Actually, the numbers I showed earlier need a small correction.

often /ˈɒfən/ adv.
Many times; frequently; in many circumstances.

Daily: I often visit the library on weekend afternoons.

Meeting: We often face this type of scope creep on similar projects.

again /əˈɡen/ adv.
One more time; returning to a previous state.

Daily: I'd love to visit that restaurant again — the food was incredible.

Meeting: Could you share that slide again so everyone can see the figures?

back /bæk/ adv.
Toward or at a previous place or position.

Daily: I'll be back by 7 PM — please start dinner without me.

Meeting: Let's take a step back and reassess our overall approach.

always /ˈɔːlweɪz/ adv.
At all times; on every occasion; forever.

Daily: She always brings homemade biscuits to the office on Fridays.

Meeting: We should always document lessons learned at the end of a project.

already /ɔːlˈredi/ adv.
Before the time expected; by this time.

Daily: I already finished reading that novel — it was incredible.

Meeting: The client has already signed off on the design. We can proceed.

really /ˈriːli/ adv.
Very; truly; used to emphasize a statement.

Daily: This coffee is really good — where did you get it?

Meeting: I really appreciate everyone making time for this call at short notice.

very /ˈveri/ adv.
To a great degree; extremely.

Daily: The weather is very warm today — perfect for a walk outside.

Meeting: I'm very pleased with the team's performance this quarter.

quite /kwaɪt/ adv.
To a considerable degree; completely.

Daily: The new café in the neighborhood is quite popular already.

Meeting: I'm quite confident we can deliver phase one by the end of next month.

only /ˈoʊnli/ adv.
Nothing more than; merely; no one else or nothing else.

Daily: I only have ten minutes before my next appointment.

Meeting: This feature is currently available only to premium subscribers.

never /ˈnevər/ adv.
Not at any time; not in any situation.

Daily: I've never been to New Zealand, but I'd love to go.

Meeting: We should never underestimate the importance of stakeholder communication.

soon /suːn/ adv.
In a short time from now; quickly.

Daily: Dinner will be ready very soon — just another ten minutes.

Meeting: I'll send over the revised proposal very soon — by end of day today.

simply /ˈsɪmpli/ adv.
In a plain way; easily; just; absolutely.

Daily: Simply add milk, stir, and you're done.

Meeting: We simply cannot move forward without senior leadership approval.

finally /ˈfaɪnəli/ adv.
At last; in the end; as the last point.

Daily: After months of searching, I finally found my dream apartment.

Meeting: Finally, I'd like to thank everyone for their hard work this quarter.

directly /dɪˈrektli/ adv.
In a straight line; without intermediaries; immediately.

Daily: You can walk directly to the beach from the hotel.

Meeting: Please send your questions directly to me before the meeting.

clearly /ˈklɪrli/ adv.
In a clear manner; obviously; without doubt.

Daily: Please speak more clearly — the connection isn't great.

Meeting: Clearly, we need to rethink our approach to customer retention.

quickly /ˈkwɪkli/ adv.
At a fast speed; with little delay.

Daily: She quickly packed her bag and ran out the door.

Meeting: We need to resolve this issue quickly before it affects the client.

easily /ˈiːzɪli/ adv.
Without difficulty; very probably.

Daily: This dish can easily be made in under twenty minutes.

Meeting: We can easily accommodate this change within the current sprint.

highly /ˈhaɪli/ adv.
Very much; to a high degree; with great praise.

Daily: She came highly recommended by three different colleagues.

Meeting: I highly recommend we review the contract terms one more time.

strongly /ˈstrɒŋli/ adv.
With great force or feeling; very much.

Daily: I strongly disagree with the direction this conversation is taking.

Meeting: I strongly recommend we bring in an external consultant for this audit.

largely /ˈlɑːrdʒli/ adv.
To a great extent; mostly.

Daily: The success of this recipe is largely due to using fresh ingredients.

Meeting: The delay is largely due to dependency on the third-party API.

generally /ˈdʒenərəli/ adv.
In most cases; as a whole; usually.

Daily: I generally prefer to cycle to work when the weather allows.

Meeting: Generally speaking, the project is progressing well.

usually /ˈjuːʒuəli/ adv.
Under normal conditions; most of the time.

Daily: I usually wake up at 6:30 without setting an alarm.

Meeting: We usually review the project status at the Monday morning standup.

certainly /ˈsɜːrtənli/ adv.
Without doubt; definitely; used to agree.

Daily: I'll certainly try that new restaurant you recommended.

Meeting: That is certainly a valid concern and worth discussing further.

particularly /pərˈtɪkjələrli/ adv.
Especially; more than usual.

Daily: I'm particularly fond of the dim sum at that restaurant.

Meeting: I'm particularly concerned about the risks in the third phase.

specifically /spɪˈsɪfɪkli/ adv.
In a precise way; for a particular purpose.

Daily: I went to that shop specifically to find that book.

Meeting: I'd like to address the budget concern specifically in this section.

recently /ˈriːsəntli/ adv.
Not long ago; lately.

Daily: I recently started learning watercolor painting — it's so relaxing.

Meeting: We recently completed a full audit of our procurement processes.

currently /ˈkɜːrəntli/ adv.
At the present time; now.

Daily: I'm currently reading a fascinating book on behavioral economics.

Meeting: We are currently evaluating three vendors for this contract.

previously /ˈpriːvɪəsli/ adv.
At an earlier time; before the present.

Daily: I've previously visited that city and loved it.

Meeting: As discussed previously, the go-live date remains the 15th of next month.

initially /ɪˈnɪʃəli/ adv.
At the beginning; at first.

Daily: I was initially nervous about the job interview, but it went well.

Meeting: The project was initially scoped for three months but has now expanded.

eventually /ɪˈventʃuəli/ adv.
At some later time; in the end.

Daily: It was a long hike, but we eventually reached the summit.

Meeting: If we keep delaying, we will eventually lose this client entirely.

approximately /əˈprɒksɪmətli/ adv.
Close to but not exactly; roughly.

Daily: The journey takes approximately forty minutes by train.

Meeting: This will require approximately 200 hours of development time.

significantly /sɪɡˈnɪfɪkəntli/ adv.
To a noticeably large degree.

Daily: My energy levels improved significantly after I started exercising.

Meeting: User engagement has increased significantly since the last update.

effectively /ɪˈfektɪvli/ adv.
In a way that produces the desired result.

Daily: You can effectively reduce stress by taking regular breaks.

Meeting: We need to communicate this change effectively to all teams.

successfully /səkˈsesfəli/ adv.
In a way that achieves the desired result.

Daily: She successfully completed a full triathlon last weekend.

Meeting: The team successfully deployed the new feature with zero downtime.

immediately /ɪˈmiːdɪətli/ adv.
Without any delay; at once.

Daily: Please call me immediately if anything goes wrong.

Meeting: We need to escalate this immediately — the client is waiting.

completely /kəmˈpliːtli/ adv.
Totally; in every way; absolutely.

Daily: I completely forgot we had plans for Sunday evening!

Meeting: This approach is completely different from what we agreed in the brief.

especially /ɪˈspeʃəli/ adv.
More than usual; in particular.

Daily: I love autumn especially for the colors and the crisp air.

Meeting: I'm especially concerned about the data security implications here.

mostly /ˈmoʊstli/ adv.
More than half; mainly.

Daily: I mostly drink herbal tea in the evenings.

Meeting: The feedback has been mostly positive, with a few minor concerns.

together /təˈɡeðər/ adv.
With each other; in combination.

Daily: We do everything together as a family.

Meeting: We need to work together across departments to solve this.

increasingly /ɪnˈkriːsɪŋli/ adv.
More and more; to a greater and greater degree.

Daily: I am increasingly interested in learning about sustainable living.

Meeting: Customers are increasingly demanding faster and more personalized service.

truly /ˈtruːli/ adv.
In a truthful way; genuinely; absolutely.

Daily: I am truly grateful for all the support I received.

Meeting: This has been a truly exceptional quarter for our organization.
