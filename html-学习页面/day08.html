Day 8 Business & Work Verbs — Professional Actions Words 351–400 · 50 words
manage /ˈmænɪdʒ/ v.
To be in charge of; to succeed despite difficulty.

Daily: She manages three different freelance projects at the same time.

Meeting: How are you managing the competing priorities across all teams?

organize /ˈɔːrɡənaɪz/ v.
To arrange or put in order; to plan and coordinate.

Daily: I spent the weekend organizing my home office space.

Meeting: Could you organize the project files in a shared folder for the team?

coordinate /koʊˈɔːrdɪneɪt/ v.
To bring different elements together to work smoothly.

Daily: I coordinate the neighborhood's annual street fair.

Meeting: Please coordinate with the design team before finalizing the specifications.

communicate /kəˈmjuːnɪkeɪt/ v.
To share or exchange information with others.

Daily: It's so important to communicate openly in a relationship.

Meeting: We need to communicate the change in strategy clearly to all stakeholders.

present /prɪˈzent/ v.
To show or give something formally; to introduce.

Daily: She presented her research findings at an international conference.

Meeting: I'll present the Q3 financial results to the board on Thursday.

propose /prəˈpoʊz/ v.
To suggest a plan or idea for consideration.

Daily: He proposed a weekend trip to the coast and everyone agreed.

Meeting: I'd like to propose a new timeline based on the updated requirements.

recommend /ˌrekəˈmend/ v.
To suggest something as worthy or beneficial.

Daily: Can you recommend a good dentist in this area?

Meeting: I strongly recommend we bring in additional QA resources for this release.

approve /əˈpruːv/ v.
To officially agree that something is acceptable.

Daily: I need to approve my son's school trip permission form.

Meeting: Has the finance director approved the revised budget proposal yet?

submit /səbˈmɪt/ v.
To formally send or present something for review.

Daily: I need to submit my tax return before the end of the month.

Meeting: Please submit all expense reports by the 28th of this month.

assign /əˈsaɪn/ v.
To give a task or responsibility to someone.

Daily: The teacher assigned a 2000-word essay for the holiday.

Meeting: Let's assign each action item to a specific owner before we close.

delegate /ˈdelɪɡeɪt/ v.
To give a task or responsibility to someone else.

Daily: Learning to delegate has improved my work-life balance enormously.

Meeting: Effective leaders know when and how to delegate tasks appropriately.

schedule /ˈskedʒuːl/ v.
To arrange for something to happen at a particular time.

Daily: I need to schedule a haircut — it's been too long!

Meeting: Can we schedule a follow-up call for later this week?

prioritize /praɪˈɒrɪtaɪz/ v.
To rank tasks in order of importance.

Daily: I try to prioritize my health above everything else.

Meeting: We need to prioritize the customer-facing issues over internal ones.

monitor /ˈmɒnɪtər/ v.
To regularly check or keep track of something.

Daily: I monitor my daily step count with my fitness tracker.

Meeting: Please monitor the server metrics closely after the deployment.

track /træk/ v.
To follow the progress or development of something.

Daily: I track my spending in a simple spreadsheet every month.

Meeting: Use the project management tool to track all open tasks and deadlines.

measure /ˈmeʒər/ v.
To determine the size, amount, or degree of something.

Daily: We need to measure the wall before buying a new bookshelf.

Meeting: How do we measure the success of this marketing campaign?

review /rɪˈvjuː/ v.
To examine and assess something carefully.

Daily: Let's review the contract together before signing anything.

Meeting: We'll review the project progress at the end of each sprint.

evaluate /ɪˈvæljueɪt/ v.
To judge the value or quality of something carefully.

Daily: We evaluated several schools before choosing one for our daughter.

Meeting: We need to evaluate each vendor against a clear set of criteria.

analyze /ˈænəlaɪz/ v.
To examine in detail to understand or explain.

Daily: I analyzed my monthly spending and found where I could save.

Meeting: Let's analyze the customer feedback data before making any product decisions.

implement /ˈɪmplɪment/ v.
To put a plan or decision into action.

Daily: She implemented a meal-planning system and never wastes food.

Meeting: We are ready to implement the new process starting from Monday.

achieve /əˈtʃiːv/ v.
To successfully reach a desired goal through effort.

Daily: She achieved her goal of running a marathon at age 50.

Meeting: What does success look like — how will we know we've achieved it?

complete /kəmˈpliːt/ v.
To finish something; to make whole or full.

Daily: I need to complete this puzzle — it's driving me crazy!

Meeting: Please complete and submit the risk assessment form by Thursday.

test /test/ v.
To check the quality, performance, or reliability of something.

Daily: The mechanic is testing the brakes before we take the long drive.

Meeting: We need at least one full week to properly test the new features.

design /dɪˈzaɪn/ v.
To plan and create something with intention and skill.

Daily: She designed her own wedding invitations from scratch.

Meeting: The UX team will design the new interface by end of next week.

launch /lɔːntʃ/ v.
To start or introduce something new to the public.

Daily: They launched a brand new brunch menu this weekend.

Meeting: We are planning to launch the new product in four target markets simultaneously.

update /ʌpˈdeɪt/ v.
To make something more recent or modern; to inform.

Daily: I update my photo albums every month to keep memories fresh.

Meeting: Could you please update the project tracker before tomorrow's standup?

maintain /meɪnˈteɪn/ v.
To keep something in its current state; to uphold.

Daily: It's important to maintain friendships even when life gets busy.

Meeting: We need to maintain a high level of quality across all deliverables.

train /treɪn/ v.
To teach someone skills; to prepare through practice.

Daily: He trains twice a week with a professional swim coach.

Meeting: All new hires will be trained on the updated compliance requirements.

hire /haɪər/ v.
To employ someone for a job; to pay for temporary use.

Daily: We hired a local guide for our hiking trip in the mountains.

Meeting: We plan to hire two additional engineers for the new product line.

negotiate /nɪˈɡoʊʃɪeɪt/ v.
To discuss and agree on terms; to find a compromise.

Daily: I successfully negotiated a discount on my apartment rent.

Meeting: The procurement team will negotiate the final contract terms next week.

confirm /kənˈfɜːrm/ v.
To establish or verify the truth of something; to agree firmly.

Daily: Can you confirm your attendance for the dinner party on Friday?

Meeting: Please confirm receipt of this email and the attached documents.

cancel /ˈkænsəl/ v.
To stop something already arranged from happening.

Daily: We had to cancel our travel plans due to the weather forecast.

Meeting: We may need to cancel the sprint demo if the feature isn't ready.

discuss /dɪˈskʌs/ v.
To talk about a topic with others.

Daily: We should discuss your career goals over lunch sometime.

Meeting: I'd like to discuss the pricing model before we send the proposal.

execute /ˈeksɪkjuːt/ v.
To carry out or perform a plan or action.

Daily: He executed the dish perfectly — every element was flawless.

Meeting: The team needs to execute this plan with precision and speed.

deliver /dɪˈlɪvər/ v.
To bring or send something; to produce the promised result.

Daily: The package was delivered right to my front door.

Meeting: Our commitment is to deliver a high-quality product on time.

address /əˈdres/ v.
To deal with or discuss something; to speak to an audience.

Daily: You need to address the issue before it becomes a bigger problem.

Meeting: I'll address your concerns about the timeline in the next slide.

resolve /rɪˈzɒlv/ v.
To settle or solve a problem or dispute.

Daily: We resolved our disagreement by calmly talking it through.

Meeting: We need to resolve this blocker before the team can move forward.

solve /sɒlv/ v.
To find an answer or solution to a problem.

Daily: My daughter loves to solve puzzles and brain teasers.

Meeting: Our team was brought together specifically to solve this technical challenge.

focus /ˈfoʊkəs/ v.
To concentrate attention or effort on something.

Daily: I use headphones to help me focus when working at home.

Meeting: Let's focus on the key deliverables for this week and nothing else.

ensure /ɪnˈʃʊər/ v.
To make certain that something happens.

Daily: I ensure I drink enough water throughout the day.

Meeting: We need to ensure all team members have access to the new system by Monday.

identify /aɪˈdentɪfaɪ/ v.
To recognize and name who or what something is.

Daily: Can you identify the bird by its call?

Meeting: Our first step is to identify the root cause of the performance issue.

establish /ɪˈstæblɪʃ/ v.
To set up; to prove or confirm something firmly.

Daily: She established her own café after years of working in hospitality.

Meeting: We need to establish clear working agreements at the start of the project.

apply /əˈplaɪ/ v.
To put to use; to make a formal request for.

Daily: I'm going to apply for a new job before the end of the year.

Meeting: Please apply the updated formatting guidelines to all client reports.

conduct /kənˈdʌkt/ v.
To carry out or organize an activity.

Daily: The teacher conducted an interesting science experiment in class.

Meeting: We will conduct a user research study before the next product iteration.

perform /pərˈfɔːrm/ v.
To carry out a task or function; to give a public presentation.

Daily: The orchestra performed beautifully at last night's concert.

Meeting: Each team member must perform a self-assessment before the review cycle.

access /ˈækses/ v.
To be able to get to or use something.

Daily: You can access all my recipes on the blog for free.

Meeting: Please make sure the entire team can access the shared drive.

process /ˈproʊses/ v.
To deal with, handle, or work through something systematically.

Daily: It takes time to process difficult emotions after a tough event.

Meeting: The system will process your request and send a confirmation within 24 hours.

handle /ˈhændəl/ v.
To manage or deal with a situation.

Daily: She handles stressful situations with remarkable calm.

Meeting: I'll handle the client communication while you focus on delivery.

enable /ɪˈneɪbəl/ v.
To make something possible; to give ability or permission.

Daily: Good sleep enables you to think more clearly throughout the day.

Meeting: This new integration will enable teams to collaborate more seamlessly.

control /kənˈtroʊl/ v.
To direct or determine how something behaves; to manage.

Daily: I find it hard to control my sweet tooth around desserts!

Meeting: We need to control costs carefully to stay within the approved budget.
