Day 19 Advanced Professional & Action Verbs Words 901–948 · 48 words
accomplish /əˈkɒmplɪʃ/ v.
To successfully achieve something.

Daily: She accomplished more in one year than most people do in five.

Meeting: What does the team need to accomplish in this sprint to stay on track?

demonstrate /ˈdemənstreɪt/ v.
To show clearly through evidence or action.

Daily: Let me demonstrate how to fold a fitted sheet properly.

Meeting: The pilot successfully demonstrated the value of this new approach.

facilitate /fəˈsɪlɪteɪt/ v.
To make something easier; to lead a discussion.

Daily: Good organization facilitates better communication between family members.

Meeting: I'll facilitate the workshop — who wants to take notes?

incorporate /ɪnˈkɔːrpəreɪt/ v.
To include as part of a whole; to take something in.

Daily: She incorporated a short walk into her lunch break every day.

Meeting: Please incorporate the legal team's feedback into the final version.

leverage /ˈlevərɪdʒ/ v.
To use something to its maximum advantage.

Daily: She leveraged her network to find a great contractor quickly.

Meeting: We should leverage our existing data before investing in new research.

align /əˈlaɪn/ v.
To put in correct position; to bring into agreement.

Daily: I need to align my expectations with reality sometimes!

Meeting: Are all teams aligned on the revised go-live date?

collaborate /kəˈlæbəreɪt/ v.
To work with others jointly on a task.

Daily: The two artists collaborated on a stunning piece for the exhibition.

Meeting: Product and engineering need to collaborate more closely on this sprint.

delegate /ˈdelɪɡeɪt/ v.
To entrust a task to another person.

Daily: Learning to delegate is the key to scaling your personal productivity.

Meeting: Not every task needs to be done by the lead — delegate where you can.

escalate /ˈeskəleɪt/ v.
To increase in intensity; to raise to a higher authority.

Daily: If a customer complaint isn't resolved quickly, it tends to escalate.

Meeting: If this issue isn't resolved in 24 hours, please escalate to the director.

negotiate /nɪˈɡoʊʃɪeɪt/ v.
To reach an agreement through discussion.

Daily: I successfully negotiated a 15% discount on the apartment rent.

Meeting: We need to negotiate the contract terms before the deadline this Friday.

mediate /ˈmiːdɪeɪt/ v.
To intervene to resolve a dispute between parties.

Daily: She mediated the disagreement between her two siblings calmly and wisely.

Meeting: Can the project manager mediate between the design and engineering teams?

allocate /ˈæləkeɪt/ v.
To distribute resources for a specific purpose.

Daily: I try to allocate at least 30 minutes per day to reading.

Meeting: We need to allocate sufficient time for testing in the project plan.

consolidate /kənˈsɒlɪdeɪt/ v.
To combine into one; to make stronger.

Daily: I consolidated all my notes into a single organized document.

Meeting: The plan is to consolidate our three regional offices into one central hub.

merge /mɜːrdʒ/ v.
To combine into one; to join together.

Daily: The two separate bus routes were merged into a single, more efficient line.

Meeting: We will merge the two product teams after the reorganization next month.

acquire /əˈkwaɪər/ v.
To gain or come to have something; to buy a company.

Daily: She acquired a taste for spicy food while traveling in Mexico.

Meeting: We are in the process of acquiring a smaller company in the AI space.

scale /skeɪl/ v.
To increase in size proportionally; to climb.

Daily: This recipe scales easily for large family gatherings.

Meeting: Our infrastructure must be able to scale to 10x current traffic.

innovate /ˈɪnəveɪt/ v.
To introduce new ideas, methods, or products.

Daily: She constantly innovates in the kitchen — every dinner is a surprise.

Meeting: We need to innovate or risk being outpaced by more agile competitors.

adapt /əˈdæpt/ v.
To make suitable for a new use; to adjust to new conditions.

Daily: My dog adapted to the new apartment surprisingly quickly.

Meeting: Our strategy must be flexible enough to adapt as the market evolves.

enhance /ɪnˈhɑːns/ v.
To improve quality, value, or attractiveness.

Daily: A good photograph can be significantly enhanced with natural light.

Meeting: This new feature will greatly enhance the user experience.

strengthen /ˈstreŋθən/ v.
To make or become stronger.

Daily: Regular walking strengthens both the body and the mind.

Meeting: Our objective is to strengthen the client relationship after last quarter's issues.

sustain /səˈsteɪn/ v.
To keep going over time; to support or nourish.

Daily: A healthy diet helps sustain energy levels throughout the day.

Meeting: We need a plan that sustains momentum beyond the initial launch phase.

balance /ˈbæləns/ v.
To keep steady; to weigh competing interests.

Daily: Learning to balance work and rest has transformed my life.

Meeting: We need to balance speed with thoroughness in our testing approach.

mitigate /ˈmɪtɪɡeɪt/ v.
To reduce the severity of something.

Daily: Deep breaths before a difficult conversation can help mitigate tension.

Meeting: What steps can we take to mitigate the risk of a delayed delivery?

capitalize /ˈkæpɪtəlaɪz/ v.
To take advantage of something; to write in capitals.

Daily: She capitalized on her language skills to secure a role abroad.

Meeting: We need to capitalize on the current market momentum before Q4.

position /pəˈzɪʃən/ v.
To put in a particular place; to present to an audience.

Daily: She positioned herself near the front to get a good view.

Meeting: How do we position this product versus our existing offering?

differentiate /ˌdɪfəˈrenʃɪeɪt/ v.
To show or make a difference between things.

Daily: Her unique writing voice differentiates her from other bloggers.

Meeting: We need to better differentiate our product from the competition.

compete /kəmˈpiːt/ v.
To strive against others for the same objective.

Daily: She competed in the local cooking competition and came second.

Meeting: To compete effectively, we need to ship features faster.

prioritize /praɪˈɒrɪtaɪz/ v.
To rank tasks in order of importance.

Daily: I've learned to prioritize self-care alongside professional goals.

Meeting: We need to prioritize the highest-impact items in the next planning session.

streamline /ˈstriːmlaɪn/ v.
To make a system more efficient and simple.

Daily: We streamlined our morning routine and saved 20 minutes every day.

Meeting: Our goal is to streamline the procurement process from 10 steps to 4.

benchmark /ˈbentʃmɑːrk/ v.
To measure against a standard for comparison.

Daily: I benchmark my running times monthly to track my improvement.

Meeting: We benchmarked our offering against the top five competitors in the market.

forecast /ˈfɔːrkæst/ v.
To predict what will happen based on current information.

Daily: The weather service forecast heavy snow for the mountain passes.

Meeting: The finance team will forecast next year's revenue by the end of November.

project /prəˈdʒekt/ v.
To estimate a future value; to present oneself confidently.

Daily: She projects calm and confidence even in high-pressure situations.

Meeting: We're projecting a 25% increase in ARR by the end of next fiscal year.

outline /ˈaʊtlaɪn/ v.
To give the main points of; to draw the outer edge.

Daily: Could you outline your idea briefly before we discuss it in detail?

Meeting: Please outline the key risks in the project plan before Thursday's review.

assess /əˈses/ v.
To evaluate or estimate the nature or quality of something.

Daily: She assessed her options carefully before resigning.

Meeting: We will assess the impact of the new pricing model over the next 60 days.

justify /ˈdʒʌstɪfaɪ/ v.
To show a good reason for; to be a good reason for.

Daily: Can you justify spending that much on a running jacket?

Meeting: We need data to justify the investment in a second development team.

anticipate /ænˈtɪsɪpeɪt/ v.
To expect or predict and prepare for something.

Daily: I always anticipate delays at the airport and arrive two hours early.

Meeting: We anticipate several questions about pricing during the client call.

coordinate /koʊˈɔːrdɪneɪt/ v.
To organize different parts so they work together.

Daily: Who's coordinating transport for the team outing next Friday?

Meeting: Please coordinate with the marketing team before making any public-facing changes.

distribute /dɪˈstrɪbjuːt/ v.
To share out among several people; to supply.

Daily: She distributed the homemade cookies equally among the children.

Meeting: Please distribute the updated slide deck to all meeting participants.

simplify /ˈsɪmplɪfaɪ/ v.
To make something easier to understand or do.

Daily: I simplified my weekly routine and felt much less stressed.

Meeting: Our goal is to simplify the expense approval process significantly.

transform /trænsˈfɔːrm/ v.
To change completely in form or appearance.

Daily: Regular exercise has completely transformed how I feel every day.

Meeting: This platform will transform the way our customers interact with our services.

optimize /ˈɒptɪmaɪz/ v.
To make the best use of a resource or opportunity.

Daily: I've been trying to optimize my sleep schedule for better energy.

Meeting: The engineering team has optimized the search query from 4 seconds to 0.3.

integrate /ˈɪntɪɡreɪt/ v.
To combine elements into a whole.

Daily: She integrated her love of travel and writing into a career.

Meeting: We need to integrate the payment gateway before the beta launch.

automate /ˈɔːtəmeɪt/ v.
To use technology to operate with minimal human input.

Daily: I automated my recurring payments so I never miss a due date.

Meeting: Automating the reporting workflow will save the team 15 hours per month.

validate /ˈvælɪdeɪt/ v.
To confirm the truth or accuracy of something.

Daily: She validated her travel booking three times before departure.

Meeting: We need real user data to validate our product assumptions.

initiate /ɪˈnɪʃɪeɪt/ v.
To begin or start something.

Daily: He was the first to initiate a conversation at the networking event.

Meeting: The project manager will initiate the formal kickoff process next Monday.

execute /ˈeksɪkjuːt/ v.
To carry out or put into action.

Daily: She executed the dish with remarkable skill and precision.

Meeting: Once we agree on the plan, we need to execute it without hesitation.

commit /kəˈmɪt/ v.
To pledge to do something; to be fully dedicated.

Daily: I've committed to running every day for the next 30 days.

Meeting: Before we commit to this vendor, we need to complete full due diligence.

deliver /dɪˈlɪvər/ v.
To bring or supply something promised; to give a speech.

Daily: The speaker delivered a powerful and emotionally moving presentation.

Meeting: We have committed to delivering version 2.0 by the end of Q2.
