Day 3 Core Nouns I — People, Time & Place Words 101–151 · 51 words
people /ˈpiːpəl/ n.
Human beings in general; a group of persons.

Daily: I love spending time with the people I care about.

Meeting: We surveyed over 500 people to gather this market data.

time /taɪm/ n.
The ongoing sequence of events; a measured period.

Daily: I never seem to have enough time to read for pleasure.

Meeting: Time is our most limited resource on this project.

year /jɪr/ n.
A period of twelve months.

Daily: We travel to the coast every year during summer.

Meeting: Revenue grew by 18% compared to the same period last year.

day /deɪ/ n.
A period of 24 hours; the hours of daylight.

Daily: I start each day with a glass of water and a stretch.

Meeting: Can we schedule the review meeting for a day next week?

way /weɪ/ n.
A method or manner of doing something; a route or path.

Daily: What's the best way to get to the train station from here?

Meeting: Is there a more efficient way to handle this approval process?

man /mæn/ n.
An adult male human being.

Daily: A kind man helped me pick up the things I'd dropped.

Meeting: He's the right man to lead the negotiations with that client.

woman /ˈwʊmən/ n.
An adult female human being.

Daily: The woman at the front desk was extremely helpful.

Meeting: She's an experienced woman in senior leadership at a global firm.

child /tʃaɪld/ n.
A young person between birth and puberty.

Daily: Every child in the neighborhood was invited to the birthday party.

Meeting: We designed this app to be simple enough for a child to navigate.

family /ˈfæmɪli/ n.
A group of people related by blood, marriage, or strong bonds.

Daily: My family always eats dinner together on Sunday evenings.

Meeting: Work-life balance is important so employees can spend time with their families.

life /laɪf/ n.
The existence of a living being; a way of living.

Daily: Life in the countryside feels slower and more peaceful.

Meeting: This product will change the daily life of millions of users.

world /wɜːrld/ n.
The earth and all the people and things on it.

Daily: I'd love to travel the world some day.

Meeting: We are a world-class team delivering world-class results.

hand /hænd/ n.
The part of the body at the end of the arm.

Daily: She raised her hand to ask the teacher a question.

Meeting: I need all hands on deck for the product launch next Friday.

place /pleɪs/ n.
A particular location, area, or position.

Daily: This café is my favorite place to work on weekday mornings.

Meeting: Is this a good place to introduce the new pricing model?

case /keɪs/ n.
A situation or example of something; a legal matter.

Daily: In most cases, the early bird gets the best seats.

Meeting: Let me walk you through a business case for this investment.

week /wiːk/ n.
A period of seven consecutive days.

Daily: I meal-prep every Sunday to save time during the week.

Meeting: We need to finalize the report by the end of this week.

night /naɪt/ n.
The period of darkness between sunset and sunrise.

Daily: I love the sound of rain at night.

Meeting: The team worked through the night to meet the launch deadline.

home /hoʊm/ n.
The place where you live; a sense of belonging.

Daily: There's no place like home after a long trip.

Meeting: We are introducing a work-from-home policy three days a week.

area /ˈeərɪə/ n.
A part of a place, region, or subject.

Daily: I live in a quiet area just outside the city center.

Meeting: We identified three key areas for improvement in the process audit.

story /ˈstɔːri/ n.
An account of events; a narrative; a floor of a building.

Daily: She told me a wonderful story about her childhood in Italy.

Meeting: Let's use a customer success story to open the presentation.

fact /fækt/ n.
Something that is known to be true or has happened.

Daily: The fact that he finished the marathon is truly inspiring.

Meeting: Let me share a few key facts to support this recommendation.

month /mʌnθ/ n.
One of the twelve divisions of the calendar year.

Daily: I visit my grandparents at least once a month.

Meeting: We will review progress at the end of each month.

study /ˈstʌdi/ n.
The activity of learning; a piece of research; a room for work.

Daily: I set aside an hour of study every evening after dinner.

Meeting: A recent study shows that flexible hours increase productivity.

book /bʊk/ n.
A set of written or printed pages bound together.

Daily: I always keep a book in my bag for commuting.

Meeting: Please book a meeting room for our three o'clock session.

eye /aɪ/ n.
The organ of sight; close attention.

Daily: She has the most striking blue eyes.

Meeting: We need to keep an eye on the budget as we scale.

job /dʒɒb/ n.
A paid position of regular employment; a task.

Daily: She landed her dream job at a technology company.

Meeting: Everyone did an excellent job on this quarter's deliverables.

word /wɜːrd/ n.
A unit of language; a promise or commitment.

Daily: I'm trying to learn a new word in English every day.

Meeting: I give you my word that the report will be ready by Friday.

friend /frend/ n.
A person you know well, trust, and like.

Daily: I'm meeting an old friend for lunch this weekend.

Meeting: Building a strong network means being a good friend to your colleagues.

power /ˈpaʊər/ n.
The ability to do something; authority or control.

Daily: The power went out during the storm last night.

Meeting: The final decision-making power rests with the executive committee.

hour /aʊər/ n.
A period of sixty minutes.

Daily: I spend about an hour at the gym every morning.

Meeting: The kickoff call will last no more than one hour.

thing /θɪŋ/ n.
An object, idea, situation, or matter.

Daily: The best thing about weekends is sleeping in.

Meeting: One important thing to note is the change in the approval workflow.

group /ɡruːp/ n.
A number of people or things that are together.

Daily: We meet as a group for book club every two weeks.

Meeting: I'd like the group to break into smaller teams for this exercise.

country /ˈkʌntri/ n.
A nation with its own land and government.

Daily: She has visited more than thirty countries in her lifetime.

Meeting: We are expanding our operations into three new countries next year.

school /skuːl/ n.
A place for teaching and learning; an institution of education.

Daily: My daughter loves her new school and has made many friends.

Meeting: We need to treat this onboarding like a first day of school.

body /ˈbɒdi/ n.
The physical structure of a person or animal; a group or organization.

Daily: Regular exercise is great for both the body and the mind.

Meeting: A regulatory body must review this change before it takes effect.

mind /maɪnd/ n.
The thinking, reasoning, and feeling part of a person.

Daily: Reading is one of the best ways to keep your mind sharp.

Meeting: Keep in mind that our Q2 targets are extremely ambitious.

plan /plæn/ n.
A decision about how to do something; a detailed scheme.

Daily: We have a plan to travel to Japan next spring.

Meeting: We need a clear plan before we begin any development work.

reason /ˈriːzən/ n.
A cause or explanation for something; logical thinking.

Daily: The main reason I moved to this city was the job opportunity.

Meeting: For this reason, I recommend we delay the launch by two weeks.

result /rɪˈzʌlt/ n.
Something that happens as a consequence of an action.

Daily: The results of the blood test came back perfectly normal.

Meeting: The results of our A/B test clearly favor the new design.

problem /ˈprɒbləm/ n.
A situation that is difficult, harmful, or needs to be solved.

Daily: The problem with this neighborhood is the lack of parking.

Meeting: We've identified the root problem and have a fix ready to deploy.

idea /aɪˈdɪə/ n.
A thought or suggestion formed in the mind.

Daily: Whose idea was it to have dinner at this amazing restaurant?

Meeting: I'd like to share a few ideas for streamlining our current workflow.

face /feɪs/ n.
The front part of the head; a surface; to deal with something.

Daily: She had a big smile on her face when she opened the gift.

Meeting: We need to face these challenges head-on rather than avoid them.

game /ɡeɪm/ n.
An activity done for fun or competition with rules.

Daily: We played a board game after dinner and laughed all evening.

Meeting: Winning this contract is a long game — we need to be patient.

city /ˈsɪti/ n.
A large and important town.

Daily: I love exploring new cities when I travel.

Meeting: We are considering opening a new office in a major Asia-Pacific city.

experience /ɪkˈspɪərɪəns/ n.
Knowledge or skill gained from doing things; an event one lives through.

Daily: Traveling alone was the most transformative experience of my life.

Meeting: We need someone with at least five years of relevant experience.

student /ˈstjuːdənt/ n.
A person who is studying at a school or university.

Daily: She was the best student in her engineering program.

Meeting: We should approach this new system as if we were students learning it.

value /ˈvæljuː/ n.
The importance or usefulness of something; a belief or principle.

Daily: Family is the thing I value most in life.

Meeting: This initiative will deliver significant value to our customers.

community /kəˈmjuːnɪti/ n.
A group of people living in the same area or sharing interests.

Daily: Our neighborhood has a very strong and supportive community.

Meeting: We want to build a thriving community around our product.

question /ˈkwestʃən/ n.
A sentence or phrase used to ask for information.

Daily: Do you have a question for the author after the talk?

Meeting: Great question — I'll address that in the next slide.

answer /ˈænsər/ n.
A response to a question; a solution to a problem.

Daily: I didn't know the answer to the last quiz question.

Meeting: Unfortunately, I don't have a definitive answer right now, but I'll follow up.

name /neɪm/ n.
A word or phrase used to identify a person, place, or thing.

Daily: What's the name of that song playing in the background?

Meeting: Please make sure your full name is on the registration form.

point /pɔɪnt/ n.
A specific idea or argument; the purpose of something.

Daily: That's a really good point — I hadn't thought of it that way.

Meeting: That's an excellent point. Let's add it to the agenda for next time.
