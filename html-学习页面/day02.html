Day 2 Core Verbs II — More Essential Verbs Words 51–101 · 51 words
understand /ˌʌndərˈstænd/ v.
To know the meaning of something; to grasp an idea fully.

Daily: I don't understand the instructions — could you explain again?

Meeting: I want to make sure everyone understands the new approval process.

watch /wɒtʃ/ v.
To look at something carefully over a period of time.

Daily: I watch the news every evening after dinner.

Meeting: Let's watch the competitor's new product launch closely.

follow /ˈfɒloʊ/ v.
To go or come after; to obey; to pay attention to.

Daily: Follow the recipe exactly for the best results.

Meeting: We need to follow the established procurement process.

stop /stɒp/ v.
To no longer continue an action; to prevent from happening.

Daily: I decided to stop drinking soda to improve my health.

Meeting: We need to stop adding scope without adjusting the timeline.

create /kriˈeɪt/ v.
To make something new; to produce or design.

Daily: She loves to create handmade cards for her friends.

Meeting: Our goal this quarter is to create a better customer onboarding experience.

speak /spiːk/ v.
To say words; to talk with someone; to give a speech.

Daily: I speak with my parents every Sunday evening.

Meeting: I'll speak to the budget constraints in my section of the presentation.

read /riːd/ v.
To look at and understand written words.

Daily: I read for at least thirty minutes before bed every night.

Meeting: Please read through the proposal carefully before the review meeting.

spend /spend/ v.
To use time or money for a purpose.

Daily: I love to spend Sunday mornings at the local farmers' market.

Meeting: We need to carefully consider how we spend the remaining budget.

grow /ɡroʊ/ v.
To increase in size, amount, or importance over time.

Daily: The tomatoes in my garden are starting to grow quickly.

Meeting: Our aim is to grow the customer base by 20% this year.

open /ˈoʊpən/ v.
To move so something is no longer closed; to start operating.

Daily: Open the window — it's stuffy in here.

Meeting: We plan to open two new regional offices before Q4.

walk /wɔːk/ v.
To move on foot at a normal pace.

Daily: I walk to the coffee shop every morning.

Meeting: Let me walk you through the key findings of the report.

win /wɪn/ v.
To succeed in a competition or achieve a desired goal.

Daily: Did your team win the football match last weekend?

Meeting: To win this contract, we need a truly compelling proposal.

offer /ˈɒfər/ v.
To present something for someone to accept or consider.

Daily: She offered to help me carry my groceries up the stairs.

Meeting: We'd like to offer a revised timeline for your consideration.

remember /rɪˈmembər/ v.
To have a memory of something from the past.

Daily: I can still remember my first day at primary school.

Meeting: Please remember to submit your expense reports by the end of the week.

love /lʌv/ v.
To have a strong feeling of affection; to enjoy greatly.

Daily: I love cooking new recipes on weekends.

Meeting: The clients love the new interface design — great work, everyone.

consider /kənˈsɪdər/ v.
To think carefully about something before deciding.

Daily: We're considering moving to a different neighborhood.

Meeting: We need to consider all the risks before finalizing the plan.

appear /əˈpɪr/ v.
To become visible; to seem to be a certain way.

Daily: A rainbow appeared after the rain stopped.

Meeting: It appears there's a discrepancy in the budget report.

buy /baɪ/ v.
To get something by paying money for it.

Daily: I need to buy a birthday present for my sister.

Meeting: We're looking to buy additional server capacity to handle peak load.

wait /weɪt/ v.
To stay in a place or do nothing until something happens.

Daily: I hate waiting in long queues at the supermarket.

Meeting: We'll have to wait for the legal team's sign-off before proceeding.

serve /sɜːrv/ v.
To help people; to provide a service; to work in a role.

Daily: This restaurant serves the best pasta in town.

Meeting: Our primary goal is to serve our customers better each quarter.

die /daɪ/ v.
To stop living; to cease to exist or function.

Daily: My phone battery dies by 3 PM every day.

Meeting: This initiative will die quickly without strong leadership support.

send /send/ v.
To cause something to go to another place or person.

Daily: Send me a text when you arrive safely.

Meeting: Please send the updated report to all stakeholders by noon.

expect /ɪkˈspekt/ v.
To think something will happen; to require as a standard.

Daily: I expect the package to arrive by Thursday.

Meeting: We expect all teams to deliver their status reports on Monday.

build /bɪld/ v.
To construct something from materials or parts.

Daily: My brother is building a treehouse for his kids.

Meeting: We are building a new internal tool to automate this process.

stay /steɪ/ v.
To remain in a place; to continue in a state.

Daily: I stayed at a wonderful hotel near the beach.

Meeting: Let's stay focused on the agenda and keep questions for the end.

fall /fɔːl/ v.
To drop down; to decrease in level or amount.

Daily: Be careful not to fall on the icy pavement.

Meeting: Sales figures fell short of our targets in the last quarter.

cut /kʌt/ v.
To divide with a sharp tool; to reduce or remove.

Daily: Can you cut the cake and serve everyone a piece?

Meeting: We need to cut costs without compromising on product quality.

reach /riːtʃ/ v.
To arrive at a place; to achieve a goal; to extend to.

Daily: It took us two hours to reach the summit.

Meeting: We're on track to reach our annual revenue target.

remain /rɪˈmeɪn/ v.
To continue to be; to stay after others have left.

Daily: A few guests remained after the party ended.

Meeting: The budget constraints remain a significant challenge going forward.

suggest /səˈdʒest/ v.
To mention an idea for someone to consider; to indicate.

Daily: I suggested trying the new pizza place on Oak Street.

Meeting: I'd like to suggest we revisit this topic in next week's meeting.

raise /reɪz/ v.
To lift or move upward; to increase; to bring up a subject.

Daily: Please raise your hand if you have a question.

Meeting: I want to raise a concern about the project timeline.

pass /pæs/ v.
To go by; to hand to someone; to succeed in a test.

Daily: Time passes so quickly when you're enjoying yourself.

Meeting: The proposal passed the initial review and is moving forward.

sell /sel/ v.
To exchange something for money.

Daily: She sells homemade jam at the weekend market.

Meeting: Our goal is to sell 10,000 units in the first month.

require /rɪˈkwaɪər/ v.
To need something; to make something obligatory.

Daily: This recipe requires fresh herbs for the best flavor.

Meeting: This process requires sign-off from both the finance and legal teams.

report /rɪˈpɔːrt/ v.
To give an account of something; to inform formally.

Daily: I report my progress to my manager every Friday.

Meeting: Please report any issues immediately to the project lead.

decide /dɪˈsaɪd/ v.
To choose between options after thinking carefully.

Daily: We decided to have a picnic instead of eating indoors.

Meeting: We need to decide on a vendor by the end of this week.

pull /pʊl/ v.
To use force to move something toward you; to attract.

Daily: Pull the door — it doesn't push open.

Meeting: We need to pull together the latest data before the presentation.

break /breɪk/ v.
To separate into pieces; to stop working; to rest briefly.

Daily: I accidentally broke my favorite coffee mug this morning.

Meeting: Let's take a ten-minute break and reconvene at 3:15.

return /rɪˈtɜːrn/ v.
To come back to a place; to give something back.

Daily: I'll return the library books before the due date.

Meeting: She will return from maternity leave on the first of next month.

receive /rɪˈsiːv/ v.
To be given, sent, or paid something.

Daily: I received a lovely birthday card from an old friend.

Meeting: Have you received the signed contract from the client yet?

allow /əˈlaʊ/ v.
To give permission for something; to make something possible.

Daily: The gym allows members to bring one guest per visit.

Meeting: The updated policy allows managers to approve expenses up to $5,000.

add /æd/ v.
To put something extra with something else; to increase.

Daily: Add a pinch of salt to improve the flavor.

Meeting: Could we add a risk section to the final project report?

manage /ˈmænɪdʒ/ v.
To be in charge of; to succeed in doing something difficult.

Daily: How do you manage to stay so calm under pressure?

Meeting: She manages a cross-functional team of twelve people.

develop /dɪˈveləp/ v.
To grow or cause to grow more advanced or complex.

Daily: I'm trying to develop a habit of reading every day.

Meeting: We plan to develop a new mobile feature in Q3.

agree /əˈɡriː/ v.
To have the same opinion; to accept a proposal.

Daily: We all agreed to meet at the park at noon.

Meeting: Can everyone agree on these key milestones before we move on?

help /help/ v.
To make it easier for someone to do something.

Daily: Could you help me carry these bags inside?

Meeting: This new tool will help the team track progress more efficiently.

support /səˈpɔːrt/ v.
To give assistance, encouragement, or resources to.

Daily: My family supports me in everything I do.

Meeting: We need the IT team to support the rollout of the new system.

produce /prəˈduːs/ v.
To make or create; to cause or generate a result.

Daily: This farm produces the most delicious organic vegetables.

Meeting: Our team produced a 40-page report in just one week.

reduce /rɪˈduːs/ v.
To make something smaller or less in amount or degree.

Daily: I'm trying to reduce my screen time before bed.

Meeting: Our objective is to reduce operational costs by 15% this fiscal year.

increase /ɪnˈkriːs/ v.
To become or make greater in size, number, or amount.

Daily: My energy levels increased after I started exercising regularly.

Meeting: We aim to increase customer satisfaction scores by Q4.

improve /ɪmˈpruːv/ v.
To make or become better in quality or performance.

Daily: I've been working hard to improve my cooking skills.

Meeting: The team's goal this quarter is to improve response time by 30%.
