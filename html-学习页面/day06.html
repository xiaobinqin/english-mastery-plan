Day 6 Core Adjectives II — More Key Descriptors Words 251–300 · 50 words
wide /waɪd/ adj.
Measuring a large distance from one side to another; broad.

Daily: The hotel room had a wide balcony with a sea view.

Meeting: We have a wide range of pricing options to suit different budgets.

deep /diːp/ adj.
Going far down or far in; intense or profound.

Daily: The lake is so clear and deep that you can see the bottom.

Meeting: This issue requires deep analysis before we draw any conclusions.

low /loʊ/ adj.
Not high or tall; below average; quiet.

Daily: I'm keeping a low profile this week and staying home.

Meeting: Our customer churn rate is at a historical low — let's keep it there.

short /ʃɔːrt/ adj.
Small in length, height, or duration.

Daily: I need a short nap — I didn't sleep well last night.

Meeting: Let's keep this update short — no more than ten minutes.

heavy /ˈhevi/ adj.
Of great weight; intense; difficult to bear.

Daily: This bag is too heavy to carry on the plane.

Meeting: The team has a heavy workload this sprint — we need to reprioritize.

fast /fæst/ adj.
Moving or happening quickly; ahead of the correct time.

Daily: She's a fast learner and picked up the software immediately.

Meeting: We need a fast solution — the client is expecting a response today.

slow /sloʊ/ adj.
Not moving or happening quickly; taking a long time.

Daily: Progress has been slow on my home renovation project.

Meeting: The approval process is too slow and is delaying the delivery.

easy /ˈiːzi/ adj.
Not difficult; requiring little effort.

Daily: Making pasta at home is much easier than people think.

Meeting: The goal is to make the onboarding process as easy as possible.

difficult /ˈdɪfɪkəlt/ adj.
Not easy; needing a lot of effort or skill.

Daily: Learning to play the piano is difficult but incredibly rewarding.

Meeting: It was a difficult decision, but we have decided to delay the launch.

positive /ˈpɒzɪtɪv/ adj.
Expressing or meaning yes; optimistic; beneficial.

Daily: Try to stay positive even when things don't go as planned.

Meeting: The initial feedback from beta users has been very positive.

negative /ˈneɡətɪv/ adj.
Expressing or meaning no; harmful; pessimistic.

Daily: Try not to let negative thoughts derail your day.

Meeting: We need to address the negative customer reviews as a priority.

specific /spɪˈsɪfɪk/ adj.
Clearly defined or identified; exact.

Daily: Do you have specific preferences for where to eat tonight?

Meeting: Could you be more specific about the requirements for this feature?

certain /ˈsɜːrtən/ adj.
Known for sure; without doubt; particular.

Daily: I'm certain I turned off the oven before leaving the house.

Meeting: I'm certain this approach will yield better results than the current one.

common /ˈkɒmən/ adj.
Occurring or found frequently; shared by many.

Daily: Catching a cold in winter is very common.

Meeting: This is a common challenge across all departments in the organization.

similar /ˈsɪmɪlər/ adj.
Resembling but not identical to something else.

Daily: Your coat looks very similar to mine — I almost picked it up!

Meeting: The approach we took last quarter was similar, but with better results.

close /kloʊs/ adj.
Near in space, time, or relationship; strict; almost equal.

Daily: My sister and I have always been very close.

Meeting: We are close to finalizing the contract with the new vendor.

ready /ˈredi/ adj.
Prepared and available for use or action.

Daily: Dinner is ready — come and sit down!

Meeting: Is the development team ready to begin testing this week?

final /ˈfaɪnəl/ adj.
Coming at the end; last; not to be changed.

Daily: The final episode of that series was absolutely unforgettable.

Meeting: Today's meeting will confirm our final decision on the vendor selection.

direct /dɪˈrekt/ adj.
Going straight from one place to another; straightforward.

Daily: There's a direct train from the city to the airport.

Meeting: I appreciate your direct feedback — it helps us improve faster.

standard /ˈstændərd/ adj.
Used or accepted as normal or average.

Daily: This is a standard recipe that you can find in any basic cookbook.

Meeting: We must ensure all deliverables meet our standard quality criteria.

official /əˈfɪʃəl/ adj.
Formally recognized or authorized.

Daily: We received the official invitation for the ceremony this morning.

Meeting: Please wait for the official announcement before sharing any news externally.

natural /ˈnætʃərəl/ adj.
Existing in nature; not made by people; normal.

Daily: I love decorating my home with natural materials like wood and stone.

Meeting: It's natural for there to be some resistance to organizational change.

physical /ˈfɪzɪkəl/ adj.
Relating to the body; relating to material things.

Daily: Regular physical activity has transformed my overall energy levels.

Meeting: We need to consider both the digital and physical aspects of the experience.

successful /səkˈsesfəl/ adj.
Having achieved an aim or goal; prosperous.

Daily: She is one of the most successful entrepreneurs in the industry.

Meeting: A successful product launch requires months of careful preparation.

effective /ɪˈfektɪv/ adj.
Producing a desired result; having a strong effect.

Daily: Exercise is the most effective way to manage stress for me.

Meeting: We need a more effective way to communicate project updates to stakeholders.

global /ˈɡloʊbəl/ adj.
Relating to the whole world.

Daily: Climate change is a global problem that requires a global solution.

Meeting: We are expanding our global footprint into three new markets this year.

international /ˌɪntərˈnæʃənəl/ adj.
Involving more than one country.

Daily: She has an impressive international travel record.

Meeting: We need to ensure compliance with international data privacy regulations.

modern /ˈmɒdərn/ adj.
Relating to the present or recent times; up to date.

Daily: I love the modern design of this new apartment complex.

Meeting: We need to modernize our legacy system to stay competitive.

traditional /trəˈdɪʃənəl/ adj.
Following long-established customs or methods.

Daily: We always have a traditional family meal on Christmas Eve.

Meeting: The traditional approach won't work in this fast-moving market.

formal /ˈfɔːrməl/ adj.
Official and following strict rules; not relaxed.

Daily: I don't own many formal outfits — just two suits.

Meeting: Please keep the language formal and professional in all client communications.

professional /prəˈfeʃənəl/ adj.
Relating to work or a paid occupation; skilled and competent.

Daily: She is a true professional in every sense of the word.

Meeting: Our team maintains a highly professional standard in all deliverables.

technical /ˈteknɪkəl/ adj.
Relating to machines, science, or technology; requiring special knowledge.

Daily: The user manual is full of technical jargon that's hard to follow.

Meeting: Can the technical team please explain the architecture in simple terms?

legal /ˈliːɡəl/ adj.
Permitted by law; relating to the law.

Daily: I need legal advice before I sign this contract.

Meeting: Please run this by the legal team before we share it with the client.

financial /faɪˈnænʃəl/ adj.
Relating to money or finances.

Daily: I've been getting better at managing my personal financial situation.

Meeting: The financial projections show strong growth potential for next year.

medical /ˈmedɪkəl/ adj.
Relating to illness and injury and their treatment.

Daily: My sister is in medical school and graduates next year.

Meeting: Please ensure all staff complete the required medical and safety training.

environmental /ɪnˌvaɪrənˈmentəl/ adj.
Relating to the natural world.

Daily: I try to make environmentally conscious choices every day.

Meeting: Our new packaging meets the latest environmental compliance standards.

practical /ˈpræktɪkəl/ adj.
Relating to actual doing; sensible and realistic.

Daily: A good backpack should be both stylish and practical.

Meeting: We need a practical plan that can actually be executed within budget.

potential /pəˈtenʃəl/ adj.
Having the capacity to become something in the future.

Daily: He's a potential candidate for the neighbourhood committee.

Meeting: We need to identify and mitigate all potential risks before launch.

necessary /ˈnesəseri/ adj.
Needed or required; essential.

Daily: Good planning is necessary if you want a stress-free holiday.

Meeting: Please include only the necessary approvals to avoid bottlenecks.

significant /sɪɡˈnɪfɪkənt/ adj.
Large enough to be noticed; important.

Daily: Getting this job was a significant turning point in my life.

Meeting: We've seen a significant increase in user engagement this month.

additional /əˈdɪʃənəl/ adj.
Added to what is already there; extra.

Daily: I packed an additional layer in case the weather changed.

Meeting: We will need additional headcount to deliver this project on time.

interesting /ˈɪntrəstɪŋ/ adj.
Holding your attention; unusual or surprising.

Daily: That documentary about ocean life was really interesting.

Meeting: That's an interesting perspective — can you elaborate on that?

serious /ˈsɪərɪəs/ adj.
Important and deserving careful thought; not joking.

Daily: Are you serious — you've never tried sushi before?

Meeting: This is a serious quality issue that we need to address urgently.

powerful /ˈpaʊərfəl/ adj.
Having great power, strength, or influence.

Daily: That speech was one of the most powerful I've ever heard.

Meeting: Data-driven decision making is a powerful tool for any organization.

likely /ˈlaɪkli/ adj.
Probable; expected to happen or be true.

Daily: It's likely to rain this afternoon, so bring an umbrella.

Meeting: It's likely we will need to adjust the timeline based on new requirements.

basic /ˈbeɪsɪk/ adj.
Forming the foundation; simple; at the lowest level.

Daily: I know enough to handle basic car maintenance.

Meeting: All new employees should complete the basic compliance training in week one.

key /kiː/ adj.
Most important; central to the purpose.

Daily: The key to happiness, I believe, is gratitude.

Meeting: The key takeaway from today's meeting is that we need more resources.

critical /ˈkrɪtɪkəl/ adj.
Extremely important; involving careful analysis; expressing disapproval.

Daily: Getting enough rest is critical to performing well under pressure.

Meeting: Meeting this milestone is critical to keeping the entire project on track.

normal /ˈnɔːrməl/ adj.
Conforming to a standard; usual, typical.

Daily: It feels good to get back to a normal daily routine.

Meeting: Response times are back to normal after this morning's system outage.

equal /ˈiːkwəl/ adj.
The same in size, quantity, or status.

Daily: Everyone deserves an equal chance regardless of background.

Meeting: We are committed to equal opportunity for all employees across the organization.
