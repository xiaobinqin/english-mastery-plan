Day 4 Core Nouns II — Organization & Work Words 151–200 · 50 words
company /ˈkʌmpəni/ n.
A business organization; a group of people.

Daily: She works for an exciting tech company in the city center.

Meeting: The company reported its strongest earnings in five years last quarter.

system /ˈsɪstəm/ n.
A set of connected things working as a whole; a method.

Daily: I use a simple filing system to keep my documents organized.

Meeting: We are migrating to a new CRM system in Q2.

program /ˈproʊɡræm/ n.
A set of instructions for a computer; a plan of activities.

Daily: I signed up for a fitness program at the local gym.

Meeting: We are launching a new employee mentorship program next month.

number /ˈnʌmbər/ n.
A quantity or amount; a figure used in counting.

Daily: I can never remember my new phone number.

Meeting: The number of open tickets has dropped by 40% this week.

government /ˈɡʌvərnmənt/ n.
The group of people who run a country or organization.

Daily: The government announced new environmental regulations last week.

Meeting: Any changes to this process require government approval.

water /ˈwɔːtər/ n.
A clear liquid essential for life.

Daily: I try to drink at least eight glasses of water every day.

Meeting: We need to water down our ambitions slightly to meet the deadline.

room /ruːm/ n.
A separate area inside a building; space or opportunity.

Daily: My favorite room in the house is the kitchen.

Meeting: There is still room for improvement in our customer support scores.

money /ˈmʌni/ n.
A means of payment; currency.

Daily: I'm saving money for a trip to Japan next year.

Meeting: We need to ensure we have enough money allocated for contingency.

business /ˈbɪznəs/ n.
Commercial activity; a company or organization.

Daily: She started her own business selling handmade jewellery online.

Meeting: Growing the business in Southeast Asia is our top priority this year.

issue /ˈɪʃuː/ n.
An important topic or problem; an edition of a publication.

Daily: The main issue with this apartment is the lack of natural light.

Meeting: We need to escalate this issue to senior management immediately.

head /hed/ n.
The top part of the body; the leader of a group.

Daily: She nodded her head in agreement.

Meeting: The department head has approved the new hiring plan.

house /haʊs/ n.
A building where people live; to contain or accommodate.

Daily: We are looking for a house with a garden for our children.

Meeting: The new data center will house our core infrastructure.

service /ˈsɜːrvɪs/ n.
The action of helping people; a system of supply.

Daily: The customer service at that hotel was absolutely outstanding.

Meeting: We need to improve our after-sales service to retain customers.

line /laɪn/ n.
A long thin mark; a row of people; a limit or boundary.

Daily: I stood in line for twenty minutes to get my coffee.

Meeting: We need to draw a clear line between the two product offerings.

order /ˈɔːrdər/ n.
A direction or command; an organized arrangement; a request.

Daily: I placed an online order and it arrived the same day.

Meeting: In order to meet the deadline, we need additional resources.

office /ˈɒfɪs/ n.
A room or building used for work; a position of authority.

Daily: I cycle to the office every morning when the weather is nice.

Meeting: The office will be closed on public holidays next month.

form /fɔːrm/ n.
A document with spaces to fill in; shape or structure; a type.

Daily: Please fill in this form with your contact details.

Meeting: In its current form, the proposal needs more supporting data.

example /ɪɡˈzæmpəl/ n.
A thing that illustrates a general rule; a model to follow.

Daily: Let me give you an example of what I mean.

Meeting: Could you share a concrete example of this happening with a client?

state /steɪt/ n.
The condition something is in; a country or its political division.

Daily: The state of my desk is an embarrassment.

Meeting: Please describe the current state of the project in your update.

level /ˈlevəl/ n.
A position on a scale; a floor of a building; a degree.

Daily: I'm an intermediate level at chess.

Meeting: The current customer satisfaction level is well below our target.

air /eər/ n.
The invisible mixture of gases surrounding the earth.

Daily: The mountain air is so fresh and invigorating.

Meeting: Let's air our concerns openly before the project begins.

process /ˈproʊses/ n.
A series of actions taken to achieve a result.

Daily: Baking sourdough is a slow and rewarding process.

Meeting: We need to streamline the approval process to reduce delays.

health /helθ/ n.
The state of being physically and mentally well.

Daily: Regular exercise is essential for good long-term health.

Meeting: Employee health and well-being are top priorities for our organization.

information /ˌɪnfərˈmeɪʃən/ n.
Facts or knowledge provided or learned about something.

Daily: I found the information I needed on the official website.

Meeting: Please share all relevant information with the team before Thursday.

team /tiːm/ n.
A group of people working together toward a common goal.

Daily: Our football team finally won the championship this year.

Meeting: The entire team deserves credit for delivering this on time.

policy /ˈpɒlɪsi/ n.
A course of action or set of rules adopted by an organization.

Daily: The hotel has a strict no-pets policy.

Meeting: The updated expense policy will take effect from the first of next month.

action /ˈækʃən/ n.
The process of doing something; a thing done.

Daily: Words are easy — action is what really matters.

Meeting: Let's agree on the action items before we close the meeting.

relationship /rɪˈleɪʃənʃɪp/ n.
The way in which two people or things are connected.

Daily: A healthy relationship is built on trust and communication.

Meeting: Building a strong relationship with this client is critical to renewal.

law /lɔː/ n.
A rule made by a government; the system of rules in a country.

Daily: There should be a law against noise after midnight.

Meeting: Any changes to this data practice must comply with local privacy law.

role /roʊl/ n.
The function or position of someone or something in a situation.

Daily: She has taken on the role of team captain this season.

Meeting: What is your role in the upcoming product launch?

part /pɑːrt/ n.
A piece or section of something; a role played.

Daily: The best part of my day is walking the dog in the evening.

Meeting: Each department will play an important part in this transformation.

decision /dɪˈsɪʒən/ n.
A choice made after thinking; a conclusion reached.

Daily: Making the decision to change careers was the best thing I ever did.

Meeting: A final decision needs to be made by Thursday to stay on schedule.

economy /ɪˈkɒnəmi/ n.
The system of money and industry in a country.

Daily: The local economy has been growing steadily for three years.

Meeting: The global economy creates both risks and opportunities for our business.

rate /reɪt/ n.
A measure of speed, frequency, or cost; a level of quality.

Daily: The crime rate in our city has fallen dramatically.

Meeting: Our customer retention rate improved significantly in Q3.

effort /ˈefərt/ n.
Physical or mental energy used to do something.

Daily: It takes a lot of effort to learn a new language.

Meeting: I want to thank the whole team for their incredible effort this quarter.

history /ˈhɪstəri/ n.
The study of past events; a record of past occurrences.

Daily: I've always been fascinated by ancient Roman history.

Meeting: This would be the biggest acquisition in our company's history.

voice /vɔɪs/ n.
Sound produced by the mouth; an expressed opinion.

Daily: She has a beautiful voice when she sings.

Meeting: Every team member should have a voice in this decision.

need /niːd/ n.
A requirement; something necessary for wellbeing.

Daily: I have a real need for more sleep in my life.

Meeting: We've identified a clear customer need that our product can address.

price /praɪs/ n.
The amount of money needed to buy something.

Daily: The price of groceries has gone up again.

Meeting: We need to review our pricing strategy against the competition.

cost /kɒst/ n.
The amount of money needed to do or make something.

Daily: The cost of living in this city is extremely high.

Meeting: We need to bring the total project cost down by 10%.

step /step/ n.
A movement of the foot; a stage in a process.

Daily: Take it one step at a time and you'll get there.

Meeting: The first step is to align on the project scope with all stakeholders.

goal /ɡoʊl/ n.
An aim or desired result; a point scored in a game.

Daily: My personal goal this year is to run a half marathon.

Meeting: Our primary goal for this quarter is to improve Net Promoter Score.

project /ˈprɒdʒekt/ n.
A planned piece of work with a specific purpose.

Daily: She's working on a creative project in her spare time.

Meeting: The project is currently on schedule and within budget.

task /tæsk/ n.
A piece of work to be done.

Daily: My biggest task today is cleaning out the garage.

Meeting: Please assign each task to a specific team member with a due date.

resource /ˈriːsɔːrs/ n.
Something useful that can be used to achieve a goal.

Daily: Time is the most valuable resource we have.

Meeting: Do we have enough resources to deliver this within the agreed timeline?

product /ˈprɒdʌkt/ n.
Something made to be sold; the result of a process.

Daily: This new product has received amazing reviews from early users.

Meeting: The product roadmap for next year will be presented next Tuesday.

market /ˈmɑːrkɪt/ n.
A place where goods are bought and sold; a group of customers.

Daily: I love shopping at the weekend farmers' market.

Meeting: Our target market is young professionals aged 25 to 35.

data /ˈdeɪtə/ n.
Facts or statistics collected for analysis.

Daily: I track my health data with a fitness app.

Meeting: Let's make sure the data is accurate before publishing the report.

report /rɪˈpɔːrt/ n.
A formal written or spoken account of something.

Daily: I read a fascinating report about climate change last night.

Meeting: The quarterly performance report will be circulated by end of Friday.

change /tʃeɪndʒ/ n.
The process of becoming different; an alteration.

Daily: A change of scenery is sometimes all you need to feel better.

Meeting: Any change to the project scope must go through the change management process.
