嘿，最近怎么样？
Very common informal greeting

早上好！又是充实的一天？（带点幽默）
How was your weekend?
周末过得怎么样？
How was your weekend? Did you manage to get some rest?

很好/安静/充实。我去爬山了/待在家里/去看家人了。
How are you holding up? It's been a crazy sprint.
你还好吧？这次冲刺真的很疯狂。

2 — Expressing Opinions & Disagreeing Constructively

I think / I feel like / In my view, [X] would work better here.
我认为/我感觉/我的观点是，X在这里会更好。
My gut says [X], but I'm open to other perspectives.
我直觉认为X，但我也愿意听其他观点。
I'm not sure I agree — can I share why?
我不确定我同意——我可以说说原因吗？
I see where you're coming from, but have you considered [X]?
我理解你的出发点，但你考虑过X吗？
That's a solid point. Building on that — what if we also [X]?
这是个好观点。在此基础上——如果我们也X呢？
I'm a bit hesitant about [X] because [reason].
我对X有点犹豫，因为[原因]。
I think we should push back on this — it could create problems downstream.
我认为我们应该反对这个——它可能会在后续造成问题。
Could we explore an alternative before committing to this approach?
在确定这个方案之前，我们能探索一下替代方案吗？
I think there's a simpler way to do this. Let me show you.
我觉得有个更简单的方法，让我演示给你看。
I totally agree — let's go with that.
我完全同意——就这样吧。
That makes a lot of sense. I hadn't thought of it that way.
这很有道理。我之前没有这样想过。
Fair point — you've convinced me.
说得对——你说服我了。
Let's agree to disagree on this one and escalate to the team.
这个问题我们意见不一，我们可以升级给团队讨论。
What does the rest of the team think?
团队其他人怎么看？
I want to flag a concern before we move forward.
在我们继续之前，我想提出一个顾虑。
Can we time-box this discussion? We've been on it for 20 minutes.
我们能给这个讨论设个时间限制吗？我们已经讨论20分钟了。

3 — Asking for Help & Clarification

Hey, do you have a minute? I'm stuck on something.
嘿，你有一分钟吗？我在某个地方卡住了。
Could you take a look at this when you get a chance?
你有空的时候能帮我看一下这个吗？
I've been banging my head against this for two hours — fresh eyes would help.
我在这个问题上纠结了两个小时了——换双眼睛看看会有帮助。
What's your take on [X]? I want a second opinion.
你对X有什么看法？我想听听第二意见。
Sorry to bother you, but I'm not sure how to approach [X].
抱歉打扰你，但我不确定如何处理X。
Can you walk me through how you solved this in [previous project]?
你能带我了解一下你在[之前项目]中是如何解决这个问题的吗？
I'm not 100% clear on the requirement — can you clarify?
我对需求不是100%清楚——你能澄清一下吗？
Just to check — you mean [X], right? Not [Y]?
确认一下——你是指X，对吧？不是Y？
Am I on the right track with this approach?
我的这个方法方向对吗？
What would you do differently if you were me?
如果你是我，你会有什么不同的做法？
I think I'm missing something here — what am I not seeing?
我觉得我在这里遗漏了什么——我忽略了什么？
Can we do a quick pair session on this? 30 minutes max.
我们能做个快速配对会话吗？最多30分钟。
Is there any documentation on this, or are you the expert?
有关于这个的文档吗，还是说你就是专家？
No worries if you're busy — I can try to figure it out myself first.
如果你忙的话没关系——我可以先自己试着解决。
Thanks for your help — that really unblocked me!
谢谢你的帮助——这真的帮我突破了卡点！
That makes total sense now. I was overcomplicating it.
现在完全明白了。我之前想复杂了。

4 — Daily Standup Meeting

昨天我处理了X。今天我继续做Y。没有阻碍。
The classic standup format
I'm currently blocked on [X]. I need [help / access / a decision] to move forward.
我目前被X阻碍了，需要[帮助/权限/决定]才能继续推进。
I finished [X] yesterday — it's in review. Today I'm picking up [Y].
昨天我完成了X，已在审查中。今天我开始做Y。
Still working on [X] — it's more complex than estimated. Should be done by [time].
仍在处理X——比预期更复杂。应该在[时间]前完成。
I merged the PR yesterday. All tests are passing. Moving on to [next task].
昨天我合并了PR，所有测试都通过了。继续做[下一个任务]。
Quick update: I resolved the [bug / issue] from yesterday. Root cause was [X].
快速更新：我解决了昨天的[缺陷/问题]，根本原因是X。
I have a blocker I want to flag — [brief description]. Can we discuss after standup?
我有个阻碍想提出来——[简短描述]。我们能在站会后讨论吗？

今天没有什么要汇报的——我上午都在开会。下午回来写代码。

I'll need [Name]'s help this afternoon with the authentication module — [Name], is that okay?

I'm on track for the sprint goal. No concerns from my side.
我的冲刺目标进展顺利，我这边没有问题。
I might need to re-estimate [task] — the complexity is higher than I initially thought.
我可能需要重新估算[任务]——复杂度比我最初预想的要高。
That's all from me. Short and sweet!
我就这些。简短精炼！

如果今天有人需要帮忙看看代码，我有空。

5 — Code Review Communication

This looks good overall. Just a couple of minor comments.
整体看起来不错，只有几个小意见。
Always start positively
Nice work! The logic here is clean and easy to follow.
干得漂亮！这里的逻辑清晰易懂。
I have a question about [X] — is this intentional, or could it cause [issue]?
我对X有个疑问——这是有意为之，还是可能导致[问题]？
Nit: could you rename this variable to something more descriptive?
小建议：这个变量能不能改个更有描述性的名字？
"Nit" = minor nitpick, not blocking
This should be blocking — [X] could cause a production issue if left this way.
这个应该阻止合并——如果保持这样，X可能会导致生产问题。
Could you add a test case for the edge case where [X]?
你能为X这个边缘情况添加一个测试用例吗？
I think there might be a more efficient way to do this — see my suggestion below.
我觉得可能有更高效的方法——参见我下面的建议。
This is a bit hard to follow — could we break this function into smaller pieces?
这个有点难以理解——我们能把这个函数拆分成更小的部分吗？
Optional: you could also handle [X] here, but it's not critical for this PR.
可选：你也可以在这里处理X，但对于这个PR来说不是关键。
LGTM — looks good to me! Approved.
LGTM——我觉得没问题！批准。
LGTM = "Looks Good To Me" — very common
Approved with minor comments — feel free to merge after addressing them.
批准，有几个小意见——处理后随时可以合并。
Could you run this past [Name] as well? They have more context on this module.
你能也让[姓名]看看吗？他们对这个模块有更多背景。
[Responding to feedback] Good catch — fixing it now.
【回应反馈】好眼力——我现在去修。
[Responding to feedback] I thought about this, and here's why I went with this approach: [reason]. Happy to discuss.
【回应反馈】我考虑过这个，以下是我选择这种方式的原因：[原因]。可以讨论。
[Responding to feedback] That's a valid concern — I'll refactor this before merging.
【回应反馈】这是个合理的顾虑，我会在合并前重构这部分。
[Responding to feedback] Done — I've addressed all the comments. Please re-review when you get a chance.
【回应反馈】完成了——我已经处理了所有意见。有空的话请重新审查。

6 — Technical Proposals & Architecture Discussion

I've been thinking about [X] and I want to propose a different approach.
我一直在思考X，我想提出一个不同的方案。
The current approach has a few limitations — here's what I'm seeing.
当前方案有几个局限性——以下是我的观察。
What if we used [technology/pattern] instead? Here's my reasoning.
如果我们改用[技术/模式]会怎样？以下是我的理由。
The trade-off is [X vs Y]. In the long term, I think [X] wins because [reason].
权衡是X还是Y。长远来看，我认为X更好，因为[原因]。
My concern with the current design is scalability — it won't hold up under heavy load.
我对当前设计的顾虑是可扩展性——在高负载下它撑不住。
I've prototyped this and it works — want to see a quick demo?
我已经做了原型，它是可行的——想看个快速演示吗？
The downside is added complexity, but the performance gain is worth it.
缺点是增加了复杂性，但性能提升是值得的。
I think we're over-engineering this. Let's keep it simple for now and optimize later.
我觉得我们过度设计了。现在保持简单，之后再优化。
What's the expected data volume in year one vs year three? That changes the answer.
第一年和第三年的预期数据量是多少？这会影响答案。
I'd like to do a spike on this before we commit — maybe one day of investigation?
在我们承诺之前，我想做个技术调研——也许一天的调研时间？
"Spike" = timeboxed exploration/research
We should document this decision and the reasoning behind it for future reference.
我们应该记录这个决定及其背后的推理，以备将来参考。
Has anyone done something similar before? I'd rather not reinvent the wheel.
有人之前做过类似的事情吗？我宁愿不重新发明轮子。
I'm not sold on this yet — can you show me some data or benchmarks?
我还没被说服——你能给我看一些数据或基准测试吗？
Let's put this in an RFC and get the whole team's input.
我们把这个写成RFC，获取整个团队的意见。
RFC = Request for Comments
That's an interesting approach. What's the risk if it doesn't work out?
这是个有趣的方法。如果行不通，风险是什么？
I'll write up a one-pager and share it before the next architecture review.
我会写一个一页纸的总结，在下次架构评审前分享。

Script A — Daily Standup (15 developers, remote)

📍 Remote video standup. Team Lead (TL) facilitates. 4 developers: Dev (backend), QA (testing), PM (product), Senior (senior engineer).
Team Lead:Morning, everyone! Let's keep it crisp — we have a hard stop at 9:15. Who wants to kick us off?
Dev:I'll go. Yesterday I finished the user profile API — it's in review. Today I'm starting on the notification service. No blockers on my end.
QA:Yesterday I worked on regression testing for the payment flow. Found two bugs — logged them both with reproduction steps. Today I'm continuing with the checkout flow tests. One blocker — I'm blocked on getting access to the staging environment. Can someone help with that after standup?
Team Lead:Sure — let's sort that out right after this. Next?
PM:Quick update from my side — I had calls with the client yesterday and got sign-off on the revised wireframes. Today I'll be updating the backlog and running the sprint planning prep. No blockers.
Senior Dev:Still working on the database migration script — it's more complex than I estimated. I should finish by end of day today. One thing I want to flag — I found a data inconsistency in the legacy records. It's not blocking me, but someone should know. Can we discuss after standup?
Team Lead:Yep — let's put that in the parking lot and come back to it. Anything else from anyone? ... Good. Let's wrap up — great work everyone, see you in the channel. [Name], ping me and we'll sort out that staging access now.
💡 Practice tip: Shadow the Team Lead's role — short, energetic sentences. Then shadow the Senior Dev's role — notice how they flag issues while keeping it brief. The standup should feel fast and focused.

Each team member should have realistic updates, one should have a blocker, and one should try to start a long discussion that I need to redirect to the parking lot."

Script B — Code Review Discussion (Giving & Receiving Feedback)

📍 Video call between a Junior Developer (JD) and Senior Developer (SD) reviewing a pull request for a new authentication feature.
Senior Dev:Hey, I went through your PR — overall this is really solid work. The structure is clean and I can see you've put thought into the error handling. I have a couple of comments I wanted to walk through with you rather than just leaving them as text.
Junior Dev:Of course — please go ahead. I'm happy to hear the feedback.
Senior Dev:So the first thing — I noticed the token validation logic is being repeated in two places. In authMiddleware.js and also in the controller. My suggestion would be to extract this into a shared utility function. It'll reduce duplication and make it easier to update in the future.
Junior Dev:That's a good point — I didn't notice the duplication. I'll refactor that now. Would it make sense to put that in the utils/auth folder?
Senior Dev:Exactly — that's the right place. Second point — I want to flag the JWT expiry time. It's set to 7 days, but our security policy requires a maximum of 24 hours. This one is blocking — we can't merge until that's changed.
Junior Dev:Good catch — I must have missed that in the security guidelines. I'll fix it right away. Should I also add a test for the expiry scenario?
Senior Dev:Yes, definitely — that's exactly the kind of edge case we should be testing. Last thing — this is just a nit — the variable name tkn isn't very readable. accessToken would be clearer. But that's minor — not blocking at all.
Junior Dev:Makes sense. I'll rename it for clarity. So to confirm — refactor the token validation, fix the expiry time, add a test, and rename the variable. Anything else?
Senior Dev:That's it! Once those are addressed, I'm happy to approve. Good PR — keep it up.
💡 Practice tip: Shadow the Senior Dev's role first — notice how every piece of critical feedback comes with a clear reason and a specific suggestion. Feedback without reasoning sounds harsh; feedback with reasoning is constructive.
🤖 AI Practice Prompt:
"Play a junior developer whose PR I'm reviewing. The PR has: (1) a duplicated function, (2) missing error handling for null inputs, (3) one excellent piece of clean refactoring I should praise. I'll play the senior reviewer. Respond to my feedback naturally — sometimes push back with good reasoning."

Script C — Technical Architecture Debate (Microservices vs. Monolith)

📍 Architecture review meeting. The team is deciding whether to split a growing monolith into microservices. PM facilitates. Two senior developers disagree.
PM:Okay, let's dig into this. We've been discussing whether to start the microservices migration this quarter. I want to hear both sides. Alex, you're in favor — can you make the case?
Alex (Senior):Sure. My argument is that the monolith is already showing scaling issues. The payment service and the user service are tightly coupled, which means a spike in one affects the other. If we break them apart now, while the codebase is still manageable, we get independent scaling, independent deployment, and we reduce the blast radius of failures.
Sam (Dev):I hear you, but I'm not convinced the pain is bad enough to justify the migration cost right now. Microservices introduce real overhead — service discovery, distributed tracing, network latency, and a whole new operations burden. I think we'd be trading one set of problems for a much harder set of problems.
Alex:That's a fair concern, but we're not talking about going full microservices overnight. What I'm proposing is a strangler fig pattern — we start extracting just the payment service, learn from that, and proceed gradually.
Sam:Okay, that changes things a bit. Extracting one service is less risky. But my concern is — do we have the operational maturity for it? We don't have a mature CI/CD pipeline, we don't have distributed tracing set up. Those need to come first.
PM:Let me jump in here — it sounds like you agree on the direction but disagree on the sequencing. Is that fair?
Alex:That's actually a good way to put it. I'm not saying do it without preparation. I'm saying let's start the preparation now, so we're ready in Q2.
Sam:Then I think we're more aligned than I thought. Let's invest in the infrastructure first — observability, CI/CD — and then revisit the extraction in Q2. I can get behind that plan.
PM:Great — so our decision is: Q1 we invest in the platform foundation. Q2 we begin the payment service extraction using the strangler pattern. I'll document this and share it for comment. Does that capture what we've agreed?
💡 Practice tip: This script is the most advanced. Shadow the PM role — notice how they reframe the disagreement as alignment at a higher level. This is a crucial facilitation skill. Also shadow Alex's role — proposing ideas confidently but staying open to pushback.
🤖 AI Practice Prompt:
"You are a skeptical senior developer in an architecture review. I'll propose we migrate to microservices. Challenge me with real technical concerns (operational complexity, team maturity, distributed systems challenges). I need to defend my proposal with reasoning, acknowledge valid concerns, and find a middle ground we can both agree on."
